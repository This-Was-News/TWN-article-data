## Article summary
Jimmy Kimmel delivered "The Alternative Christmas Message" to U.K. viewers on Channel 4, using the platform to criticize U.S. President Donald Trump and warn about the rise of fascism. Kimmel, who had recently returned to his show after a suspension, joked that it had been a "great year" for fascism and that "tyranny is booming" in the U.S. He referenced Trump's actions and rhetoric, suggesting the president acts like a king and has been involved in disputes with the media. Kimmel highlighted the importance of free speech, noting that many people spoke up for it during his show's suspension. He expressed that the U.S. is "tearing down the structures of our democracy" and apologized to the U.K. for the country's current "wobble," urging them not to give up on the U.S. Channel 4's alternative message tradition has previously featured figures like Edward Snowden and Mahmoud Ahmadinejad. Kimmel's message touched upon the "special relationship" between the U.S. and U.K., despite historical divides.
