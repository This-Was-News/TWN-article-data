## Article summary
NPR's Pop Culture Happy Hour podcast hosts debate the worst Christmas movies ever made, offering a humorous and critical look at holiday films that miss the mark. Stephen Thompson singles out "Jingle All The Way" (1996) for its commercialism and stretched-out, unfunny plot centered on a man's quest for a toy. Aisha Harris finds Netflix's "I Believe in Santa" (2022) to be a creepy exception to her usual tolerance for bad Christmas rom-coms, questioning its premise of a man who genuinely believes Santa is real. Wailin Wong expresses her long-held resentment for "Love Actually" (2003), criticizing its numerous sad storylines, fatphobic humor, and the narcissism of Andrew Lincoln's character's grand romantic gesture. Glen Weldon discusses "Scrooge and Marley" (2012), a low-budget gay adaptation of "A Christmas Carol," acknowledging its sincerity despite his personal aversion to sentimentality.
