## Article summary
Jane Goodall, the renowned primatologist and conservationist, has passed away at the age of 91. Known for her groundbreaking research on chimpanzees in Africa, Goodall's discoveries revolutionized scientific understanding of animal behavior and emotions. She documented chimpanzees using tools and exhibiting complex social structures, challenging the notion that such behaviors were exclusive to humans. Goodall's work transformed public perception of animals and propelled her into global advocacy for environmental protection. She traveled extensively, speaking to audiences about conservation and climate change, often balancing grim realities with messages of hope. Goodall was honored with numerous awards, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2025. Her influence extended beyond science into education and humanitarian causes. She founded the Jane Goodall Institute and initiated the Roots & Shoots program to empower young people in conservation efforts. Goodall's legacy includes her unwavering commitment to protecting the natural world and promoting a more compassionate understanding of animals. Her passing has been met with tributes from leaders and organizations worldwide, recognizing her extraordinary contributions to humanity and the planet.
