## Article summary
The funeral of former Vice President Dick Cheney at Washington National Cathedral was attended by a bipartisan group of notable figures, including former presidents and vice presidents. George W. Bush, who served with Cheney, delivered a eulogy praising him as "solid and rare and reliable." Joe Biden, who succeeded Cheney as vice president, remembered him as a "decent man" and recalled receiving helpful advice. Vice President Kamala Harris and former Vice President Mike Pence were also in attendance, with Pence sharing that Cheney had advised him to "do the right thing" regarding the 2020 election results. Former Vice Presidents Al Gore and Dan Quayle were present as well. Notably absent were current President Donald Trump and Vice President JD Vance, who were not invited. Trump had been a vocal critic of Cheney and his daughter, Liz Cheney. Former Democratic presidents Bill Clinton and Barack Obama were invited but did not attend.
