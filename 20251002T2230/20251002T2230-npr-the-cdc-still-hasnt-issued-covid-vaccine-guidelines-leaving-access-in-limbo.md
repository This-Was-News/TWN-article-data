## Article summary
Parents like Remy Sweeney-Garrett are struggling to get their children vaccinated against COVID-19 due to the CDC's delay in issuing final guidelines. The CDC's lag is unusual and has caused confusion among the public and healthcare providers. Dr. Susan Kansagra, chief medical officer for the Association of State and Territorial Health Officials, highlights the widespread confusion. Dr. Elias Kass, the Sweeney-Garrett family's doctor, expresses frustration at the lack of vaccines and clear guidelines. The delay has also affected adults seeking vaccination, creating a confusing patchwork of state rules. Dorit Reiss, a vaccine policy expert, criticizes the CDC's inaction, predicting more COVID-19 cases as a result. The Department of Health and Human Services did not respond to requests for comment. This year's vaccine rollout has been chaotic, with the FDA restricting shots to high-risk individuals, causing confusion. The CDC's Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices, influenced by anti-vaccine members, recommended changes but the CDC has not acted. The government shutdown adds to the uncertainty. Officials are concerned about the upcoming winter respiratory season, with Dr. Philip Huang expressing frustration over the delays.
