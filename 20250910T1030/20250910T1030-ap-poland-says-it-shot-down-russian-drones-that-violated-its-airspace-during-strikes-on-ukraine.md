## Article summary
Poland reported that multiple Russian drones violated its airspace during a wave of Russian strikes on Ukraine, describing the incident as an act of aggression. Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk stated that the drones were shot down with help from NATO allies. European leaders, including EU foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas, expressed concerns that Russia is intentionally escalating the war. Defense Minister Władysław Kosnak-Kamysz confirmed that over 10 objects entered Polish airspace, with NATO's Air Command and the Royal Netherlands Air and Space Force providing support. This incident marks the most serious violation of European airspace by Russia since the war began. Leaders from Baltic states like Lithuania, Latvia, and Estonia expressed deep concerns about Russian aggression. A house in the village of Wyrzyk was hit by a missile or drone, but no injuries were reported. The Polish armed forces are conducting a search for possible crash sites and have urged the public to avoid any suspicious objects. Warsaw's Chopin Airport temporarily suspended flights due to the closure of airspace. NATO confirmed its air defenses supported Poland, and Dutch F-35 fighter jets stationed in Poland under NATO provided assistance. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy highlighted the importance of the European aircraft's intervention, emphasizing a strong response to Russian escalation. Meanwhile, Ukraine reported intercepting or jamming numerous drones and missiles, with several regions experiencing damage and casualties.
