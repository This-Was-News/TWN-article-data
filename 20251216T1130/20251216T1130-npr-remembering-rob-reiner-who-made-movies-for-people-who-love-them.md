## Article summary
Rob Reiner, a director known for making movies that audiences deeply connect with, has passed away. His filmography includes iconic works such as "When Harry Met Sally...", a film that significantly influenced the romantic comedy genre, and "Stand By Me", a poignant coming-of-age story. "This Is Spinal Tap" is recognized as an early and influential mockumentary, while "Misery" earned Kathy Bates an Oscar. "The American President" is noted as a precursor to "The West Wing", and "A Few Good Men" is remembered for its famous catchphrase. "The Princess Bride" also contributed memorable lines that have become cultural touchstones. Reiner possessed a remarkable talent for comedic timing, creating vivid atmospheres, and directing a range of genres from absurdist comedy to dramatic thrillers. His films were not just popular or well-made; they became personal favorites for many, fostering strong emotional attachments and becoming subjects of frequent discussion and quoting. This ability to create enduring connections with audiences, exemplified by films like "The Sure Thing", highlights Reiner's legacy as a director who made movies that people not only watched but truly loved and cherished.
