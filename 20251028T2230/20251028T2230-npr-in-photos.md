## Article summary
Hurricane Melissa, a Category 5 storm with winds of 185 miles per hour, has made landfall in Jamaica. This powerful storm is expected to be one of the most significant in Atlantic history. The western part of Jamaica is experiencing intense winds and a storm surge that could reach 13 feet, with catastrophic damage anticipated. Residents in Jamaica and Cuba have been seen preparing for the storm, with some evacuating to safer locations and others securing their homes and belongings. Images capture the approaching storm, with high waves crashing on beaches and people walking through flooded streets. Volunteers in Miami are assembling relief packages in anticipation of the storm's impact. The hurricane's rapid intensification and immense strength have prompted widespread concern and preparations across the affected regions.
