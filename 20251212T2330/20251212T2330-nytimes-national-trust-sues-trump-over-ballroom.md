## Article summary
The Trump administration is facing multiple lawsuits and controversies. A federal appeals court has temporarily halted a contempt inquiry into deportation flights of Venezuelan immigrants. The National Trust for Historic Preservation is suing to stop the construction of a large ballroom on White House grounds, alleging violations of at least four laws. The Justice Department is suing Fulton County, Georgia, to seize and inspect 2020 election ballots, continuing to promote false claims of election fraud. The administration is also providing air passenger data to immigration agents to expand deportation efforts and has ordered the National Park Service to remove merchandise promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion from its gift shops. Furthermore, the Transportation Department is threatening to withhold highway funds from New York over commercial licenses issued to noncitizen truckers. House Democrats released new photos from Jeffrey Epstein's estate, highlighting his ties to powerful men, including President Trump and former President Bill Clinton. President Trump announced that leaders of Thailand and Cambodia agreed to a cease-fire in their border conflict. The Navy has submitted a report to the Defense Secretary regarding Senator Mark Kelly's participation in a video advising military members to refuse unlawful orders. In other news, President Trump presented the 1980 U.S. Olympic men's hockey team with Congressional Gold Medals, and the administration removed sanctions on a Brazilian Supreme Court justice and his wife.
