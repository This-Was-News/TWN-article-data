## Article summary
President Donald Trump announced plans to deploy the National Guard to Memphis to address crime concerns, with the support of local authorities. This move follows similar deployments in Los Angeles and Washington. Meanwhile, the Pentagon has implemented a zero-tolerance policy for military personnel making inappropriate posts about the killing of conservative activist Charlie Kirk, with several investigations underway. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent urged G7 nations to impose tariffs on countries purchasing oil from Russia to pressure an end to the war in Ukraine. In other news, DC Public Schools will restrict spectators at football games to adults or students with adult chaperones due to recent conflicts. U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio criticized China's plan to establish a nature reserve at Scarborough Shoal, a disputed territory in the South China Sea. Missouri Republicans approved a redistricting plan that could help them win an additional U.S. House seat, but opponents plan a referendum. President Trump also discussed his decision to send troops to Memphis after advice from Union Pacific CEO Jim Vena, who highlighted crime issues in the city. Additionally, Trump approved federal disaster aid for six states and tribes affected by recent storms and floods, though delays in approval have been noted. The summary also touches on Trump's push to revitalize American manufacturing, which has faced challenges due to immigration crackdowns, and his comments on various political and social issues.
