## Article summary
Authorities have arrested 22-year-old Tyler Robinson for the killing of conservative activist Charlie Kirk. Utah Governor Spencer Cox announced the arrest, stating that a family member tipped off authorities after Robinson allegedly confessed. FBI Director Kash Patel confirmed the arrest, praising the collaborative effort. Investigators are examining evidence, including bullet casings with inscribed messages, to understand Robinson's motives. The gun used in the shooting was a Mauser bolt-action rifle. Cox mentioned that Robinson had become more political recently and expressed hatred towards Kirk. Neighbors described Robinson's family as normal and hardworking. Governor Cox warned about the dangers of political violence, urging society to choose peace over escalation. Kirk was shot while speaking at Utah Valley University, sparking a massive manhunt. The suspect was captured roughly 33 hours after the shooting, faster than in a similar recent case.
