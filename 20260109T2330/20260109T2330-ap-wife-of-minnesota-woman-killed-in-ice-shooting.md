## Article summary
The wife of Renee Good, a woman fatally shot by a U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement officer in Minneapolis, has broken her silence, stating that she and her wife had "whistles" while the officers had "guns." Becca Good described her wife, Renee, as a kind, sparkling mother of three who was a U.S. citizen and had no criminal record beyond a traffic ticket. Trump administration officials characterized Renee Good as a domestic terrorist who attempted to run over an officer, a claim rejected by state and local officials and protesters. Becca Good emphasized that they were raising their son to believe in compassion and kindness for everyone, regardless of their background. She expressed her commitment to continuing Renee's legacy of teaching their son that people are building a better world. The couple had recently moved to Minneapolis and were raising Renee's 6-year-old son from a previous marriage. Renee Good was described as a poet, writer, wife, and mom who embraced Minneapolis. Her ex-husband confirmed he never knew her to participate in protests. Becca Good thanked supporters and stated that those who committed the act were driven by fear and anger, and that a better way must be shown to them.
