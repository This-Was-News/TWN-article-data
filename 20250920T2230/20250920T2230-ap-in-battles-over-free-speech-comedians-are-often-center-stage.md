## Article summary
The article discusses the role of comedians in free speech battles, highlighting the suspension of Jimmy Kimmel and comparing it to the experiences of other comedians like Bassem Youssef, who fled Egypt due to political pressure. Comedians often face backlash from powerful figures, serving as a bellwether for societal freedom. Kimmel's suspension followed controversial comments about a political assassination, sparking debates over free speech. Comedians and late-night hosts have rallied in support of Kimmel, emphasizing the importance of free expression. The article also mentions international examples of censorship, such as the suppression of comedy in Russia, Iran, and India. It notes that while Kimmel's situation is not as extreme as in authoritarian regimes, it shares similarities with broader trends of media control. The piece concludes by discussing the shift from 'cancel culture' to a 'consequence culture,' with mixed reactions from conservatives and comedians.
