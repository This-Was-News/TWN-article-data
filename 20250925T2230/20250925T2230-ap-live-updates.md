## Article summary
President Donald Trump signed an executive order allowing TikTok to continue operating in the U.S. under American ownership, with Chinese President Xi Jinping's approval. Concerns about data security and Chinese influence on the platform have been addressed through a deal involving U.S. investors like Oracle and Silver Lake Partners. Meanwhile, Trump discussed various topics, including crime in Washington D.C., potential tariff revenue for farmers, and the U.S.-China trade dispute. He also met with Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdogan to discuss military equipment sales and other bilateral issues. Additionally, Trump addressed the U.S. economy's rebound, the situation in Ukraine, and the upcoming Ryder Cup, where he will be present despite past controversies. Other news includes a shooting at an ICE facility, potential impeachment of Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr., and former Chilean President Michelle Bachelet's plans to run for U.N. Secretary-General.
