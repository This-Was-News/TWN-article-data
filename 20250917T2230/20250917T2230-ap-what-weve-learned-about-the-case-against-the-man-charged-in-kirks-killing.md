## Article summary
Students returned to Utah Valley University following the fatal shooting of conservative activist Charlie Kirk. Authorities arrested Tyler Robinson, 22, charging him with aggravated murder and other crimes. Robinson surrendered after negotiations, fearing a SWAT team confrontation. Prosecutors believe Robinson targeted Kirk due to his political views. Investigators found Robinson's DNA on the rifle used in the killing, which belonged to his grandfather. Robinson left a note expressing his intent to kill Kirk, whom he criticized for his hatred. Authorities are investigating whether Robinson acted alone. The university is reviewing security measures for Kirk's event. Kirk was known for his combative conservative views, criticizing racial justice movements and LGBTQ+ rights. Robinson's family reported a shift in his political views, becoming more supportive of gay and transgender rights. The case remains under investigation, with authorities gathering more details about Robinson's actions and motivations.
