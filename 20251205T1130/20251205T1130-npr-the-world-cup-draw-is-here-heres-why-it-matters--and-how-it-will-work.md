## Article summary
The 2026 FIFA World Cup draw is a highly anticipated event that will determine the initial group stage opponents for all 48 participating teams. The tournament, hosted across the U.S., Canada, and Mexico, is the largest in its history, with teams divided into 12 groups of four. While 42 nations have qualified, six spots remain, to be decided in upcoming playoffs. Teams are placed into four pots based on FIFA rankings, with specific rules governing group compositions to ensure a balanced competition. Notably, top-ranked teams like Spain and Argentina, and France and England, are strategically placed to potentially avoid meeting until the final stages. The draw itself will not reveal the specific match locations or times, which will be determined at a separate event. This draw significantly influences a team's path to the knockout rounds, with all teams aiming to avoid the "Group of Death." Despite the unpredictability of the World Cup, powerhouses like Spain, England, Argentina, and Brazil are considered early favorites.
