## Article summary
The White House East Wing, built in 1942 and housing offices for the first lady and staff, began demolition on Monday, October 23, 2025. This demolition is to make way for President Donald Trump's new $250 million ballroom. The East Wing has served various functions throughout its history, including hosting presidential family events, press briefings, and serving as offices for numerous staff members. Archival photos showcase its past, from a 1913 wedding ceremony to holiday decorations in 2016 and meetings in the first lady's office in 2007. The construction of the West Wing in 1902 is also referenced. The demolition process is visually documented, showing the ongoing work and debris. The East Wing has been a site for significant events, including presidential family gatherings like watching the Super Bowl in 1993 and official ceremonies such as swearing in Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen in 2021. It has also been a space for first ladies to meet with dignitaries and for displaying children's artwork. The transformation of this historic part of the White House marks a significant change in its architectural landscape.
