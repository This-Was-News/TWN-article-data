## Article summary
Global health leaders gathered at the U.N. General Assembly following significant U.S. foreign aid cuts, creating a tense atmosphere. Solomon Zewdu of The END Fund noted anxiety and a lack of coordinated dialogue among attendees. He emphasized the urgency of addressing global health needs. Varnee Murugan of the U.S. Chamber of Commerce expressed optimism about the new U.S. global health strategy, highlighting the potential of private-sector involvement. Atul Satija of the Nudge Institute observed deeper conversations about global problems and innovative solutions. Peter Sands of the Global Fund described the event as a 'high wire' moment, balancing funding cuts with scientific advancements. Jackie Aldrette of AVSI-USA felt a renewed energy and collaboration despite the U.S. aid reductions, noting that the global community continued to function without U.S. leadership.
