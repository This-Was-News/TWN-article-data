## Article summary
Trinidad and Tobago's Prime Minister Kamla Persad-Bissessar has revised her earlier statement, confirming that U.S. Marines are currently in the country working on the airport's radar, runway, and road on the island of Tobago. She stated that this collaboration aims to enhance surveillance and intelligence capabilities for detecting narco-traffickers. This development follows recent U.S. military activity in the Caribbean, including ongoing strikes against suspected drug traffickers and a visit from U.S. General Dan Caine to Trinidad and Tobago. While the U.S. government's specific plans for the radar at the Tobago airport remain unclear, officials have confirmed a U.S. military plane recently landed on the island for refueling. Earlier, the Dominican Republic granted the U.S. temporary access to facilities to aid in the fight against drug trafficking.
