## Article summary
David Gilmour, the legendary guitarist and singer of Pink Floyd, is celebrating his 80th birthday next year with a flurry of new projects. He recently released a solo album, *Luck and Strange*, followed by a concert film and an upcoming live album, *The Luck and Strange Concerts*. This comes as Pink Floyd's iconic 1975 album, *Wish You Were Here*, marks its 50th anniversary with a deluxe edition featuring demos and live recordings. In a recent interview, Gilmour reflects on the pressures of following up *The Dark Side of the Moon* and shares insights into the creative process behind *Wish You Were Here*. He discusses the album's title and the challenges of maintaining inspiration amidst global turmoil. Gilmour also highlights the joy of collaborating with his family, including his daughter Romany, who joined him on tour. The interview delves into his understated guitar style and his lifelong passion for music, emphasizing his commitment to the art over fame or fortune. Gilmour's latest work continues to showcase his enduring talent and creativity.
