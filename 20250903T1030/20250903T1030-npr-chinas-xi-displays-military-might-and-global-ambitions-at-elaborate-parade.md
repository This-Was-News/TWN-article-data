## Article summary
China's President Xi Jinping hosted a grand military parade in Beijing on September 3, 2025, marking the 80th anniversary of victory over Japan in World War II. The event showcased China's military strength and global ambitions, with thousands of soldiers marching through Tiananmen Square. Over two dozen world leaders attended, including Russian President Vladimir Putin and North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, marking the first time these leaders of the 'new axis of upheaval' gathered at a single event. Xi's speech emphasized China's unwavering stance against intimidation and highlighted the choice between peace and war, dialogue and confrontation. The parade featured advanced military hardware, including warplanes, bombers, drones, and new nuclear missiles, signaling China's growing military capabilities. The event was seen as a diplomatic win for China, demonstrating its alliances and resistance to U.S. pressure. President Trump criticized the gathering, accusing the leaders of conspiring against the United States. The parade also marked Kim Jong Un's first attendance at an international multilateral event, underscoring China's influence. Public opinion in China supports close ties with Russia and North Korea, with surveys showing high favorability towards these nations. The parade concluded with a spectacular display of military aircraft, leaving spectators inspired and proud of China's strength.
