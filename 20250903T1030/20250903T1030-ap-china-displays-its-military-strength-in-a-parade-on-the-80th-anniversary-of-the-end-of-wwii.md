## Article summary
China showcased its military might in a parade marking the 80th anniversary of the end of World War II, with Chinese leader Xi Jinping and guests, including Russian President Vladimir Putin and North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, in attendance. The event highlighted China's progress and military advancements, including aerial and underwater drones, hypersonic missiles, and fighter jets. Xi emphasized China's strength and self-reliance, stating that the Chinese people are not afraid of violence and are determined to safeguard their sovereignty. The parade also featured an 80-gun artillery salute and formations spelling out the number 80. U.S. President Donald Trump commented on the parade, questioning whether Xi would acknowledge American contributions to the war. The event underscored China's growing influence and its role as a stabilizing force in an unstable world, with analysts noting the opportunity for China to fill political, economic, and military vacuums.
