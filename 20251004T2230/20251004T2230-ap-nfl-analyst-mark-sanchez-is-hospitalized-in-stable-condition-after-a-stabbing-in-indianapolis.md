## Article summary
Former NFL quarterback and current Fox Sports analyst Mark Sanchez was stabbed in an altercation in downtown Indianapolis and was hospitalized on Saturday. Sanchez was in Indianapolis to call the Raiders-Colts game and was in stable condition. Indianapolis police are investigating the incident, which occurred around 12:30 a.m. Saturday between two men, one of whom was hospitalized with stab wounds. The other man received treatment for lacerations. The confrontation took place in the popular Wholesale District next to the Indiana statehouse. Police reviewed video footage and stated it was an isolated incident between the two men. Fox Sports expressed gratitude to the medical team and asked for privacy for Sanchez and his family. Sanchez, 38, had a 10-year NFL career before retiring in 2019. He played for the Jets, Eagles, Cowboys, and Washington, finishing with 15,357 passing yards and 86 touchdowns. The Jets and former teammates posted messages of support on social media.
