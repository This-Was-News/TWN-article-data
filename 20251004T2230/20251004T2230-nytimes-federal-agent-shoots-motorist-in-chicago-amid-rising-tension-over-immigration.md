## Article summary
A federal agent shot and wounded a motorist in Chicago during a confrontation, sparking protests over immigration enforcement. The incident occurred on Saturday morning, with the wounded individual suffering non-life-threatening injuries. Federal officials stated that the motorist, who was armed, attempted to ram a law enforcement vehicle. The Chicago Police Department responded to the scene. The clash drew dozens of protesters to the area, who chanted against federal immigration activity. Federal agents used pepper balls to disperse the crowd, and Chicago Police officers tried to calm tensions. The incident is part of escalating immigration enforcement in Chicago and its suburbs. The story is developing, with updates expected. Julie Bosman and Hamed Aleaziz reported for The New York Times.
