## Article summary
Sophie Kinsella, the beloved author known for the "Shopaholic" book series, has passed away at the age of 55. Her real name was Madeleine Sophie Wickham. Last year, she revealed her diagnosis with an aggressive form of brain cancer in 2022. Kinsella's death was announced on Instagram, with her family sharing that she faced her illness with immense courage and felt blessed for her loved ones and career success. Her "Shopaholic" novels achieved global popularity, selling tens of millions of copies and being translated into numerous languages. The first two books in the series inspired the 2009 film "Confessions of a Shopaholic," starring Isla Fisher. In a 2019 NPR interview, Kinsella discussed her focus on young women's journeys in her novels, noting the excitement of that life stage filled with career and romantic possibilities. She found inspiration in the potential of new encounters and the open horizon of the future.
