## Article summary
President Donald Trump has arrived in Japan for his first Asia trip of his current term, with a primary focus on trade and peace efforts. He is scheduled to meet with Japan's new Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi, a protege of the late Shinzo Abe. A potential trade deal between the U.S. and China is nearing finalization, with negotiators discussing tariffs, rare earths, and agricultural purchases. Trump's visit comes amid a prolonged U.S. government shutdown, impacting federal workers and services. Japan has welcomed Trump with patriotic displays in Tokyo, including lighting up landmarks in red, white, and blue. The president also addressed ongoing renovations at the Kennedy Center and the Oval Office via social media. Trump announced the end of trade negotiations with Canada due to a provincial government advertisement critical of his tariffs. He also expressed a desire for Federal Reserve Chairman Jay Powell to leave his position. The president highlighted his administration's transparency while remaining secretive about a donor who funded U.S. troops during the shutdown. He also claimed credit for Argentine President Javier Milei's electoral success and discussed potential U.S. beef purchases from Argentina. Trump reconnected with Elon Musk, suggesting their past dispute was resolved. He has not ruled out a third term but dismissed the idea of running for vice president. The president reiterated his willingness to meet with North Korean leader Kim Jong Un if an opportunity arises. Tensions with South Korea over immigration raids were addressed, with plans for a new visa system. Trump previously attended the ASEAN summit in Malaysia, where he helped broker a ceasefire between Thailand and Cambodia and advanced U.S.-China trade talks.
