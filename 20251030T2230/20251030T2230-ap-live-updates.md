## Article summary
The Trump administration has drastically reduced the number of refugees the U.S. will admit to 7,500 for the 2026 fiscal year, a significant decrease from the previous administration's cap of 125,000. The new policy prioritizes mostly white South Africans, a decision criticized by Democratic lawmakers as illegal and morally indefensible. The administration has also faced criticism for its handling of the SNAP program, with a coalition of cities and organizations filing a lawsuit to prevent benefits from being halted due to the government shutdown. Military families are experiencing significant mental toll due to the ongoing shutdown, uncertain about their paychecks. In other news, the U.S. stock market is near record highs despite ongoing trade tensions with China. The White House is also planning a Halloween event, and there are concerns about potential misuse of federal authority regarding a proposed White House ballroom. The State Department has ordered non-essential personnel to leave Mali due to terrorism threats and a fuel crisis. The U.N. Secretary-General has criticized President Trump's announcement to restart nuclear weapons testing. Additionally, the Treasury Department has sanctioned a human smuggling organization, and the Justice Department is investigating Black Lives Matter leaders for alleged fraud. California's Democratic senators are demanding the cancellation of federal election observers in the state, citing partisan motivations linked to President Trump's statements. The Trump administration is also moving to eject some nonprofits from a student loan forgiveness program if their work is deemed to have a substantial illegal purpose.
