## Article summary
Nuno F.G. Loureiro, a 47-year-old MIT professor specializing in nuclear science and engineering, was fatally shot at his home in Brookline, Massachusetts. Authorities have launched a homicide investigation, but no suspects have been taken into custody. Loureiro, who joined MIT in 2016, was a physicist and fusion scientist leading the Plasma Science and Fusion Center, with a focus on advancing clean energy technology. He was widely admired for his mentorship and contributions to science. The president of MIT described his death as a shocking loss. The investigation is ongoing, and there is currently no known connection to a recent shooting at Brown University in Rhode Island. Neighbors reported hearing loud noises on the night of the incident. Loureiro's colleagues and students have expressed their grief and remembered his passion for fusion energy as a transformative force for humanity.
