## Article summary
The article discusses the impact of President Donald Trump's travel ban on international students aiming to study in the United States. Bahara Saghar, an Afghan student, had planned to study business administration in Illinois but was affected by the travel ban. Thousands of students from 19 countries face obstacles due to visa application delays and stricter vetting processes. Some students, like Pouya Karam from Iran, have deferred their admissions or are advocating for changes. The travel ban, affecting citizens from 12 countries, has led to significant disruptions in students' plans. In Myanmar, a student named Gu Gu had his plans to study at the University of South Florida ruined by the travel ban. Many students are now considering alternatives in Europe but face additional challenges. The article highlights the personal and emotional toll on students who have invested time and money into their U.S. education dreams. Some students, like Amr from Iran, have had to postpone or cancel their plans to study or work in the U.S. The overall sentiment is one of frustration and uncertainty among international students affected by the travel ban.
