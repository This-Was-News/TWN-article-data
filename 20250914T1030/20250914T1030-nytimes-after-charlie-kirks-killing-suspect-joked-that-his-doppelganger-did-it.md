## Article summary
After the killing of Charlie Kirk, suspect Tyler Robinson, 22, was arrested. Before his arrest, Robinson sent messages to friends showing he was closely following news about the killing. An acquaintance tagged him in a group chat with surveillance images of the suspect, to which Robinson joked that his doppelganger was trying to get him in trouble. Another user joked that Robinson had killed Kirk. Robinson was arrested nearly 34 hours after the shooting. The Discord messages provided insight into Robinson's demeanor post-killing. Robinson's messages did not reveal a motive for the shooting, which has been debated politically. He joked about the reward for the suspect and suggested disposing of a manifesto and rifle. Robinson's messages indicated he was following news reports closely. Police stated Robinson confessed to the crime and surrendered to authorities. After his arrest, group chat members struggled to believe he was accused of the crime.
