## Article summary
A memorial service for Charlie Kirk, a polarizing conservative activist assassinated less than two weeks ago, was held at State Farm Stadium in Glendale, Arizona. The event drew tens of thousands of attendees, including President Trump, Vice President JD Vance, and other high-ranking officials and conservative figures. The service blended Christian devotion with political fervor, with speakers portraying Kirk as a martyr and a hero for the conservative movement. The event featured fiery speeches, including one by Stephen Miller, who warned of Kirk's political enemies. Attendees included a mix of conservative celebrities, Trump administration members, and notable figures from the right. Security was heightened, with the Department of Homeland Security designating the event as a top-level security priority. President Trump and Elon Musk were seen together at the memorial, marking a reconciliation after their public feud. The service also included an altar call and testimonies from Kirk's widow, Erika Kirk, who urged attendees to follow her husband's example in marriage and fatherhood. The event aimed to both honor Kirk and galvanize the conservative political movement in his name.
