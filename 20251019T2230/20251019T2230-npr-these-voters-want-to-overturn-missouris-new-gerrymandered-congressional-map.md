## Article summary
In Missouri, thousands of voters are protesting the state legislature's gerrymandered congressional map, which favors the GOP. Leann Villaluz is leading efforts to gather signatures for a petition that would allow voters to decide the map's fate. The new map, signed into law by Governor Mike Kehoe, targets Democratic Rep. Emanuel Cleaver II's seat. People Not Politicians Missouri aims to overturn the map through a referendum, having already collected over 100,000 signatures. The group faces legal challenges from state officials, including a lawsuit from Attorney General Catherine Hanaway. The Democratic National Committee is supporting the referendum effort, contributing staff and funds. Voters like Rebeca Amezcua-Hogan express concern about diluted voting power and reduced representation. Despite pushback, Villaluz and others continue to rally support, believing Missouri voters will ultimately decide the map's future.
