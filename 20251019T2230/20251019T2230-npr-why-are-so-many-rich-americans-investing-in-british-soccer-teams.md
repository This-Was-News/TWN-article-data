## Article summary
Many wealthy Americans are investing in British soccer teams due to their affordability and the high level of competition. Teams like Bournemouth, Liverpool, and Chelsea are now owned by Americans, who appreciate the rich history and passionate fan base of British soccer. The involvement of U.S. celebrities like Will Ferrell, Snoop Dogg, and LeBron James has further boosted interest. TV shows like 'Ted Lasso' have also increased American fascination with British soccer. American owners are making the teams more commercial and investing in women's football. Despite fears of major changes, owners like Bournemouth's Jim Frevola assure that significant overhauls are not planned. Fans in Britain generally support American ownership, noting improvements in stadiums and team performance. The growing American audience for British soccer, now at 40 million, is expected to increase further with events like the upcoming World Cup in the USA.
