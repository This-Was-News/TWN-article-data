## Article summary
President Trump discussed recent military strikes against drug smugglers in the Caribbean and Pacific, claiming they save American lives. The administration imposed new sanctions on Russia's largest oil companies, Lukoil and Rosneft, following a pause in sanctions during Trump's second term. Secretary of State Marco Rubio is traveling to Israel, Malaysia, South Korea, and Japan for diplomatic summits. Trump falsely claimed that grocery prices are down and dismissed negotiations with Democrats over healthcare funding. He is demolishing the White House East Wing to build a ballroom, funded by private donors and himself. The administration's policy of killing suspected drug smugglers on boats has expanded to the Pacific, with critics arguing it is illegal. Senator Jeff Merkley is delivering a marathon speech protesting Trump's policies. The University of Virginia struck a deal with the Trump administration to avoid a federal investigation. Representative-elect Adelita Grijalva filed a lawsuit to be sworn in to Congress. Vice President JD Vance visited Israel to support the Gaza cease-fire. A man was arrested after crashing a vehicle into a security gate near the White House. Federal agents conducted a raid in Lower Manhattan, detaining several African immigrants suspected of selling counterfeit goods.
