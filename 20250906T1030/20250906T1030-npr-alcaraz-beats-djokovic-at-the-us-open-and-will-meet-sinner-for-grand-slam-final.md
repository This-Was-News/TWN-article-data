## Article summary
Carlos Alcaraz defeated Novak Djokovic in the U.S. Open semifinals, showcasing his youth, athleticism, and creativity. Alcaraz will face Jannik Sinner in the final, marking the first time the same two players have met in three consecutive Grand Slam finals within one season. Sinner and Alcaraz have dominated the past eight major trophies, with Alcaraz holding five Slam titles and Sinner four. The final will also decide the world No. 1 ranking, with President Donald Trump planning to attend. Alcaraz has been on a winning streak, reaching the finals in his last eight tour-level events. Sinner aims to become the first repeat men's champion at the U.S. Open since Roger Federer. Djokovic, despite his experience, was unable to keep up with the younger players, acknowledging the physical challenge of competing against them. Both Alcaraz and Sinner expressed mutual respect and familiarity with each other's playing styles. Alcaraz's journey includes a notable win over Djokovic, whom he admires for his legendary career.
