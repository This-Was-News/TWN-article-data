## Article summary
The Justice Department is investigating mortgage fraud allegations against Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook, with subpoenas issued as part of the probe. Meanwhile, a contentious Senate hearing focused on recent upheavals at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), with Robert F. Kennedy Jr. clashing with Dr. Susan Monarez, who was abruptly removed from her post. The U.S. imposed sanctions on three Palestinian human rights organizations for their alleged involvement in efforts by the International Criminal Court to investigate Israel. Former President Joe Biden underwent Mohs surgery for a skin lesion, following previous health issues. Chicago Latino leaders urged peaceful protests during Mexican Independence Day celebrations, citing concerns over potential federal troop deployments. Republican Senate candidate Mike Rogers called for Detroit Mayor Mike Duggan to seek President Trump's help in reducing violent crime. The Trump administration is asking the Supreme Court to allow the president to fire a member of the Federal Trade Commission. A federal appeals court temporarily halted the shutdown of a controversial immigration detention center in the Florida Everglades. The Trump administration plans to demolish a section of the White House to build a new ballroom without approval from the commission tasked with overseeing federal building construction. Massachusetts will require insurance carriers to cover vaccinations recommended by the state's health department, regardless of federal recommendations. First Lady Melania Trump called for investment in AI training for American schoolchildren. Secretary of State Marco Rubio reiterated that the war in Gaza could end if Hamas disarmed and released hostages, while also addressing concerns about humanitarian suffering. Senator Roger Marshall criticized the number of vaccines required for U.S. children, holding up a sign with '76 Jabs!' during a hearing. Vice President JD Vance criticized senators for their behavior during Kennedy's hearing. A federal judge ordered the Trump administration to release billions of dollars in foreign aid funding approved by Congress. Rubio challenged the findings of the latest U.N. World Drug Report, asserting that Venezuela plays a significant role in drug smuggling. The developer of an offshore wind farm that would power 350,000 homes accused the Trump administration of stopping the nearly completed project. Stephen Moore, President Trump's pick to join the Federal Reserve board, disclosed that he would remain a White House employee even if confirmed. Senator Tina Smith questioned Kennedy's stance on vaccines, contrasting his assertions during confirmation with his statements on a podcast. Senator Bill Cassidy sought to balance his support for President Trump with his medical expertise, questioning Kennedy's anti-vaccine stances. French President Emmanuel Macron and European leaders dialed up Trump after hybrid talks on post-war security guarantees for Ukraine. Senator John Barrasso expressed concern that trust in vaccines could be jeopardized under Kennedy's leadership. Senator Maggie Hassan accused Kennedy of restricting COVID-19 vaccines while applauding their initial development. Senator Maria Cantwell questioned Kennedy about his ties to Jeffrey Epstein and his decision-making processes. The District of Columbia sued to stop Trump's deployment of the National Guard for law enforcement intervention. Senator Mike Crapo discussed the impact of the Republican tax and spending law on rural hospitals, with Kennedy responding that the law increased funding for rural communities. Nonpartisan estimates suggest that roughly 10 million people are expected to lose health insurance due to the legislation, disproportionately affecting rural areas.
