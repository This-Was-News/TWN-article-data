## Article summary
A small Mexican Navy plane, on a medical mission to transport a child, crashed near Galveston, Texas, resulting in the deaths of at least five people. The aircraft was carrying four Navy officers and four civilians, including a child, and was associated with a nonprofit that aids children with severe burns. The cause of the crash is currently under investigation, with the FAA and NTSB on the scene. A witness described a harrowing rescue attempt in thick fog and jet fuel fumes. The Mexican Navy expressed condolences, and the nonprofit reaffirmed its commitment to care for burn victims. The crash occurred in Galveston Bay, prompting a search and rescue operation. Weather conditions, including heavy fog, may have been a factor. The incident highlights the risks involved in medical transport missions.
