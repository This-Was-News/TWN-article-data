## Article summary
The Consumer Electronics Show (CES) in Las Vegas prominently featured robots and artificial intelligence, signaling a significant trend in the tech industry. AI is moving from the cloud into hardware, with a particular focus on robotics, including humanoid and manufacturing-focused models. Companies like Boston Dynamics, LG, and Roborock showcased advanced robots, highlighting the convergence of AI and physical robotics for commercial deployment. The event also emphasized the growing importance of AI in everyday products, suggesting that nearly every company is becoming an AI company. A notable innovation was the Nemonic Dot, a speech-to-braille label printer for the visually impaired. The health industry, termed 'digital health,' was another major focus, with advancements in wearable medical devices expected to drive substantial market growth. Products like the Withings Smart Scale and Oura's medical rings aim to provide users with more detailed health insights. While data privacy concerns were raised regarding health tech, the overall trend points towards increased data collection and AI-driven insights for personal health management. CES 2026 indicated a strong trajectory for AI and robotics, shaping future consumer trends and technological advancements.
