## Article summary
Millions of low-income Americans are facing uncertainty regarding their food assistance benefits as the new month begins. Christine Tully, a 78-year-old retiree, expressed her confusion and worry about not receiving her usual $285 in monthly aid from the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP). A federal judge in Rhode Island ordered the Trump administration to continue funding food stamps during the shutdown, ruling that the administration's refusal to use emergency funds was unlawful. President Trump stated that the administration would provide funding for November's food stamps after a federal court clarifies how the money can be legally supplied, but he indicated that a delay is likely. The situation leaves many, like Ms. Tully, anxious about how they will afford groceries.
