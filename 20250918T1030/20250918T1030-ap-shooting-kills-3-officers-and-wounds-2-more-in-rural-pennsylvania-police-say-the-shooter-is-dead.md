## Article summary
A shooting in rural York County, Pennsylvania, killed three officers and wounded two more during a domestic-related investigation. The shooter was killed by police. The community held a procession to honor the fallen officers, with officials condemning the violence. This incident is one of the deadliest for Pennsylvania police this century. The investigation will cover multiple locations, and the identities of the shooter and officers involved have not been released. The two injured officers are in critical but stable condition. The shooting occurred near North Codorus Township, about 115 miles west of Philadelphia. Neighbors reported hearing multiple shots and seeing a heavy police presence. The area is rural, with farms and fields nearby. Family members of the deceased officers are grieving but proud. Another officer in the area was killed in February during a hospital hostage situation.
