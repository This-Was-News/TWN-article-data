## Article summary
FBI Director Kash Patel defended the Trump administration's handling of Jeffrey Epstein files during a contentious hearing with the House Judiciary Committee. Democrats criticized Patel for not releasing more files, with Rep. Jamie Raskin questioning his transparency. Rep. Eric Swalwell had a heated exchange with Patel over whether Trump's name was in the Epstein files. Patel insisted the FBI had released all legally permissible files and pointed to judicial rulings. Patel also mentioned investigating a potentially forged letter to Epstein bearing Trump's name. The hearing featured fiery exchanges, including a shouting match between Patel and Swalwell. Democrats praised the FBI's role in Operation Viper in Memphis, while Republicans focused on the Trump-Russia investigation. Patel called the investigation's launch a scandal but confirmed the FBI is examining various aspects of it. The hearing highlighted deep political divisions and ongoing investigations into past controversies.
