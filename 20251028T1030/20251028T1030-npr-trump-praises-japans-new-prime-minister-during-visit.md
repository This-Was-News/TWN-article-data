## Article summary
President Donald Trump met with Japan's new Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi during his Asia trip, praising her as the country's first female leader. The two leaders discussed trade, with Trump aiming to secure $550 billion in Japanese investment. Takaichi offered gifts and expressed a desire to strengthen ties, referencing former Prime Minister Shinzo Abe's relationship with Trump. They signed agreements on trade, reaffirming an alliance framework, and securing critical minerals. Takaichi also indicated she would nominate Trump for the Nobel Peace Prize. The leaders visited a U.S. aircraft carrier, where Trump spoke to troops and Takaichi mentioned Toyota's investment in America. Trump's visit also touched on efforts to end the war in Ukraine and included a ceremonial meeting with the emperor. Earlier, Trump had participated in an ASEAN summit and facilitated a ceasefire between Thailand and Cambodia. Tensions with China appeared to be easing ahead of a planned meeting between Trump and Chinese leader Xi Jinping.
