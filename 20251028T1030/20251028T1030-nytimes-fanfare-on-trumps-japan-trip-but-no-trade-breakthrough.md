## Article summary
President Trump met with Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi in Tokyo, praising the strong alliance between the two nations and vowing to assist Japan with any needs. They signed agreements on shipbuilding and cooperating on a rare earth metals supply chain, aiming to reduce reliance on China. Trump also addressed U.S. troops at Yokosuka Naval Base, discussing military strategy and domestic issues. Throughout his Asia trip, Trump expressed a desire to meet with North Korean leader Kim Jong-un, suggesting a meeting could occur in South Korea. The U.S. and Japan also discussed Japan's promised $550 billion investment in the U.S. and the ongoing issue of Japanese citizens abducted by North Korea. Prime Minister Takaichi reportedly nominated Trump for a Nobel Peace Prize. The visit occurred during a U.S. government shutdown, raising questions about troop paychecks. Trump's remarks to the troops included political rally-like rhetoric and comments on their appearance.
