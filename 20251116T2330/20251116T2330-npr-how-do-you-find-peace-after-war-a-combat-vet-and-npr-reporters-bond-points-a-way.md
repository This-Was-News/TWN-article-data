## Article summary
NPR Veterans Correspondent Quil Lawrence's decade-long reporting on Dave Carlson, an Iraq war veteran, chronicles his arduous journey from combat and incarceration to redemption and a new life. Carlson, who served two tours in Iraq and struggled with PTSD, battled addiction and legal troubles, leading to his incarceration. During his time in prison, he found solace and a new perspective through religion and a desire to help others. Upon his release, Carlson faced significant challenges in reintegrating into civilian life, including relapses and struggles with his newfound responsibilities as a father. His wife, Alicia, played a crucial role in his recovery, supporting him through rehabilitation and his pursuit of education. Carlson eventually enrolled in law school, inspired by a desire to mentor troubled youth and demonstrate that one can be a warrior with discipline and compassion. He successfully navigated a volatile situation involving domestic violence, showcasing his ability to apply his military training constructively without resorting to violence. Carlson ultimately passed the bar exam and became a practicing attorney, finding a new mission and a sense of purpose. His story highlights the complex and long-term impact of war, the importance of community and support networks in recovery, and the possibility of finding peace and rebuilding a life after trauma.
