## Article summary
John F. Kennedy's granddaughter, Tatiana Schlossberg, has revealed that she is terminally ill with acute myeloid leukemia. Diagnosed in May 2024 at age 34, she has undergone chemotherapy and two stem cell transplants. One of her doctors has indicated she may only have about a year left to live. Schlossberg, an environmental journalist and daughter of Caroline Kennedy, shared her diagnosis in an essay published on the 62nd anniversary of her grandfather's assassination. In her essay, she also criticized policies advocated by her cousin, Robert F. Kennedy Jr., specifically mentioning his stance on mRNA vaccines. Schlossberg expressed fears about her young children not remembering her and sadness over not being able to continue her life with her husband. She also noted the pain her diagnosis has caused her family, particularly her mother. Her essay details her medical journey, including participation in clinical trials, and her doctor's prognosis. The article also includes related stories about RFK Jr.'s views on science and vaccines.
