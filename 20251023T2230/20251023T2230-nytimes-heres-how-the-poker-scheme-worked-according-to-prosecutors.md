## Article summary
Federal authorities unveiled charges of illegal gambling involving mafia members and former professional basketball players who collaborated to rig poker games. Mafia members organized and secured the games, while former athletes like Chauncey Billups and Damon Jones recruited wealthy players. The games were rigged using sophisticated technology, including card-reading machines and hidden cameras. Cheaters communicated using secret signals and specialized contact lenses or sunglasses. Losing players faced threats of violence and extortion to repay their debts. The scheme involved multiple mafia families operating in various locations, including Manhattan, the Hamptons, Las Vegas, and Miami. The case highlights the use of advanced technology and organized crime in illegal gambling operations.
