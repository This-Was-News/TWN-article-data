## Article summary
The FBI has released photos of a person of interest in the shooting of Charlie Kirk, who was assassinated at Utah Valley University. The suspect is believed to have fled after firing a single shot and has not yet been identified. Authorities recovered a high-powered rifle near the scene. The assassination has drawn bipartisan condemnation and highlighted the escalating threat of political violence in the U.S. Investigators are appealing to the public for tips and are pursuing multiple leads. President Donald Trump expressed condolences and hopes for the swift capture of the shooter. Security experts have questioned whether the event was sufficiently staffed, noting that outdoor venues and campus police forces have limitations. The FBI is offering a $100,000 reward for information leading to the identification of the shooter. Political leaders across the spectrum have canceled engagements due to security concerns, and there have been calls for unity and an end to political violence. Investigators are confident they will find the shooter and are analyzing evidence, including video footage and a partial fingerprint.
