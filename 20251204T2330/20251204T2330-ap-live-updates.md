## Article summary
Navy Admiral Frank Mitch Bradley stated there was no "kill them all" order regarding an attack on an alleged drug boat near Venezuela, though lawmakers remain concerned, particularly after viewing video footage of two survivors being killed. Democrats expressed grave concerns, with Rep. Adam Smith stating the order was to "Destroy the drugs, kill the 11 people on the boat." Legal experts suggest the attack could be a war crime if survivors were intentionally targeted. The Supreme Court allowed Texas's challenged congressional redistricting plan to be used in the upcoming election, a map favorable to Republicans. Kenyan President William Ruto urged President Trump to support efforts to resolve the humanitarian crisis in Sudan. Investigative reports revealed significant costs and injuries from Navy mishaps during operations against Houthi rebels, including a friendly fire incident and a collision. The President is preparing to depart the White House for the annual National Christmas Tree lighting ceremony. Several lawmakers called for the release of video footage and legal opinions related to the boat attack.
