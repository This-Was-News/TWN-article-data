## Article summary
World leaders gathered at the United Nations to criticize its flaws and urge reforms, highlighting its outdated structures and ineffectiveness. The critiques focused on the U.N.'s inability to address global issues, inequitable representation in the Security Council, and the dominance of powerful nations. Leaders from various countries, including India, Guinea, and Malaysia, expressed dissatisfaction with the U.N.'s performance. U.S. President Donald Trump also criticized the U.N. for not living up to its potential and for ineffective actions. Despite the criticism, leaders acknowledged the U.N.'s importance and the need for member states to reform it. The U.N. was described as an aspirational institution, embodying the dream of global cooperation. The critiques were seen as a way to push for necessary changes and improve the organization's credibility and trustworthiness. The discussion also touched on the U.N.'s historical context, warning against repeating the failures of the League of Nations. Overall, the leaders emphasized the need for the U.N. to adapt and become more effective in addressing global challenges.
