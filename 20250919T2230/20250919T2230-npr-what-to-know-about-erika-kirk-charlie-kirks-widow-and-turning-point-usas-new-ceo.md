## Article summary
Erika Kirk, a faith-based entrepreneur and widow of late conservative activist Charlie Kirk, has been unanimously elected as the new CEO and board chair of Turning Point USA, the right-wing youth organization her husband founded. Erika, 36, is known for advocating traditional views on gender and marriage, encouraging women to prioritize family over career. She has pledged to continue her husband's mission, including the organization's campus tour and annual AmericaFest conference. Turning Point USA has seen a surge in interest following Charlie Kirk's assassination, with over 62,000 requests from students to start or join chapters. Erika, a former college basketball player and pageant queen, holds degrees in political science, international relations, and is pursuing a doctorate in biblical studies. She runs a Christian clothing line and hosts a devotional podcast. The couple met in 2018, married in 2021, and had two children. Erika has vowed to continue her husband's legacy, urging young people to join or start Turning Point USA chapters. The organization's board has vowed to make Charlie's work 'more powerful and enduring than ever before.'
