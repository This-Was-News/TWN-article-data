## Article summary
Many wealthy Americans are investing in British soccer teams, drawn by the affordability and prestige of the Premier League. Teams like Bournemouth, Liverpool, and Chelsea are now owned by Americans, who appreciate the deep history and passionate fan base of British soccer. The involvement of U.S. celebrities like Will Ferrell, Snoop Dogg, and LeBron James has further boosted interest. TV shows like 'Ted Lasso' have also increased American fascination with British soccer. American owners are making teams more commercial and investing in women's football. Despite fears of major changes, owners like Bournemouth's Jim Frevola assure that significant overhauls are not planned. Fans in Bournemouth generally support their American owner, noting improvements in the stadium and team foundations.
