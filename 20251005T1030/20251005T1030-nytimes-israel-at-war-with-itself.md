## Article summary
The article discusses the deep divisions and isolation Israel faces amid the ongoing Gaza war, highlighting the trauma and debates within Israeli society. The war has intensified distrust and hatred, with Israel's military actions in Gaza drawing global criticism and accusations of genocide. The conflict has also exacerbated internal divisions, with a growing religious movement advocating for Israeli control over all historic Jewish lands, while secular liberals view this as a threat to Israel's democratic values. The article explores the personal stories of those affected, including hostages and their families, and the broader geopolitical implications, including President Trump's proposed plan for Gaza. The war has left both Israelis and Palestinians deeply scarred, with little hope for a peaceful resolution in sight.
