## Article summary
Several new state laws are taking effect on January 1, 2026, impacting various aspects of daily life. In California, rideshare drivers now have the right to unionize, a significant development for gig economy workers. Colorado is expanding paid family leave for parents with babies in the NICU, offering an additional 12 weeks of paid time off. Virginia has implemented a law limiting social media use for individuals under 16 to one hour per day, though it faces a legal challenge. Eighteen states will now restrict the use of SNAP benefits for purchasing candy and sodas, aiming to promote healthier eating habits. Minnesota is launching a comprehensive paid family and medical leave program, providing up to 20 weeks of partial pay. Illinois is regulating the use of artificial intelligence in employment decisions, prohibiting its use if it considers demographic information. These laws reflect a growing trend of state-level legislation addressing labor rights, child welfare, public health, and the ethical implications of emerging technologies.
