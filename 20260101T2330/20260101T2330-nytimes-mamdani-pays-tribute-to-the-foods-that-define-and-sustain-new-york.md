## Article summary
In his inaugural speech as mayor, Zohran Mamdani celebrated New York City by highlighting its diverse food culture, from bodega sandwiches to chicken biryani. He emphasized food's role in economic well-being, proposing city-owned supermarkets to improve affordability. Mamdani views food as a source of identity, spiritual sustenance, and unity, recalling his childhood experiences with New York's culinary offerings like powdered doughnuts and Koronet Pizza. He extended an invitation to New Yorkers to share his affection for their city, describing it as a unique place where diverse cultures and flavors coexist. Mamdani invoked the contributions of those who work in the city, including subway operators, park workers, and food vendors, acknowledging their vital role in sustaining New York. He reminisced about formative moments tied to food, such as eating bagels and lox every Sunday as a child. The mayor's speech underscored the idea that loving New York means cherishing its unparalleled cultural tapestry, exemplified by its varied sounds, smells, and tastes. He used vivid examples like the steelpan, sancocho, and coffee prices to illustrate the city's distinctiveness. Mamdani's tribute to food serves as a metaphor for his vision of a more inclusive and equitable New York.
