## Article summary
Zohran Mamdani has been sworn in as the mayor of New York City, becoming the first Muslim mayor of the city. He took his oath of office on a Quran at a decommissioned subway station. Mamdani, a Democrat, promised to govern "expansively and audaciously," emphasizing his commitment to using government power to benefit the working class. He plans to implement policies such as free child care, free buses, and a rent freeze. Mamdani, who is also of South Asian descent and the first mayor born in Africa, is the city's youngest mayor in generations. His inauguration was attended by notable figures like U.S. Sen. Bernie Sanders and former Mayor Bill de Blasio. Mamdani's platform centers on affordability and addressing the city's high cost of living. He aims to raise taxes on the wealthy to fund social programs. Despite facing potential challenges and disagreements, supporters express optimism about his leadership and unifying potential for the city.
