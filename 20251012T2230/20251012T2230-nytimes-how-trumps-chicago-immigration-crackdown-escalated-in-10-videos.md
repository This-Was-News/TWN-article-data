## Article summary
The New York Times article details the escalating tensions in Chicago due to the Trump administration's immigration crackdown. Initially, the operation began with increased arrests, but it quickly intensified, leading to confrontations between federal agents and residents. Videos captured protests, raids, and arrests, showing the rising tensions. A fatal shooting of an unauthorized immigrant by immigration officers heightened the situation. Federal agents used tear gas and pepper balls against protesters, including clergy members. Residents aggressively pushed back, chasing agents and recording their actions. The crackdown, initially expected to last 45 days, has no clear end in sight, leaving Chicagoans on edge. The article highlights the city's response, including large protests and confrontations, as tensions continue to escalate.
