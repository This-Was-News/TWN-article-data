## Article summary
Diane Keaton, known for her unique charm and eccentricity, has had a remarkable career spanning five decades. She often played unconventional roles, defying Hollywood norms. Her breakout role as Kay Adams in 'The Godfather' showcased her depth and ability to hold her own alongside more flashy counterparts. 'Annie Hall,' her most iconic role, highlighted her quirks and insecurities, becoming a quintessential film. 'Looking for Mr. Goodbar' further cemented her versatility, playing a complex character with a double life. 'Reds' demonstrated her ability to handle dramatic and intense roles, despite challenging working conditions. 'Baby Boom' offered a commentary on modern life, blending comedy and heartwarming moments. 'Something's Gotta Give' broke stereotypes by featuring a 50-something woman as the lead in a romantic comedy. Keaton's performances are marked by her ability to channel her personal struggles into compelling and relatable characters.
