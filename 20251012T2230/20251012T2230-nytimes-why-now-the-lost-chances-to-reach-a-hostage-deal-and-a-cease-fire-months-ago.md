## Article summary
The article discusses the delayed hostage deal and cease-fire between Israel and Hamas, questioning why it took 736 days. It highlights the role of President Trump in pushing for the deal, contrasting his approach with that of former President Biden. The piece explores missed opportunities for a cease-fire, including after the death of Hamas leader Yahya Sinwar and during a January 2025 cease-fire that ultimately failed. It also examines the changing dynamics in 2025, including Hamas' leadership crisis and Israel's military advantages. The article emphasizes Trump's unconventional diplomatic style and his influence on Prime Minister Netanyahu, leading to the eventual hostage release and cease-fire agreement. It concludes by noting the public's support for ending the war to bring the hostages home.
