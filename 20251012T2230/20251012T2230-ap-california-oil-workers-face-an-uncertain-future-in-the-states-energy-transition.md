## Article summary
California's energy transition is causing uncertainty for oil workers. Willie Cruz, who worked at a refinery that shut down, transitioned to respiratory therapy. His son, Wilfredo, faces job loss as the Phillips 66 refinery in Los Angeles closes. Thousands of workers may lose jobs as California reduces fossil fuel reliance. State Democrats are addressing job losses and high gas prices. California's oil production has declined, and refinery closures impact the economy. Phillips 66 and Valero refineries are closing, affecting refining capacity. State programs aim to retrain workers, but funding is limited. Advocates and industry leaders debate the future of oil workers. Wilfredo Cruz is preparing for a career change but feels uncertain about the transition.
