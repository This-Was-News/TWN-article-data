## Article summary
Diane Keaton, the Oscar-winning star known for her roles in 'Annie Hall,' 'The Godfather,' and 'Father of the Bride,' has passed away at the age of 79. Reports indicate she died in California surrounded by loved ones. Keaton was celebrated for her unique acting style and iconic performances, which spanned decades. She won an Oscar for 'Annie Hall' and was nominated three more times. Keaton also collaborated with filmmaker Nancy Meyers on several beloved films. Born Diane Hall, she adopted the stage name Keaton and studied under Sanford Meisner. She began her career on Broadway and made her film debut in 'Lovers and Other Strangers.' Keaton was also romantically linked to several notable figures, including Woody Allen and Al Pacino. She adopted two children later in life and continued acting steadily throughout her career. Keaton was honored with an AFI Life Achievement Award in 2017 and left a lasting legacy in Hollywood.
