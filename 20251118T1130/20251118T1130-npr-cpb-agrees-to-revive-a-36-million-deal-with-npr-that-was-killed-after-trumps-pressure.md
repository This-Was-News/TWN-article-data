## Article summary
The Corporation for Public Broadcasting (CPB) has agreed to reinstate a $36 million contract with NPR, which had been previously canceled due to pressure from the Trump White House. This decision resolves a lawsuit filed by NPR, alleging that CPB illegally succumbed to political demands to financially penalize NPR for its news coverage. The original contract was for NPR's operation of a satellite distribution system for local public radio stations. CPB had initially awarded the contract to a new consortium, Public Media Infrastructure (PMI), claiming a desire for faster digital innovation, a defense the judge found not credible. NPR's President and CEO, Katherine Maher, hailed the settlement as a victory for editorial independence and First Amendment rights. CPB, while not admitting wrongdoing, stated that the settlement ends costly litigation and supports public media's future through PMI. Federal subsidies for public broadcasting have ceased, impacting NPR, PBS, and many local stations.
