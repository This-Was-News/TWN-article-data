## Article summary
Ukrainian energy workers are bravely continuing their essential duties, repairing and maintaining the nation's power infrastructure amidst ongoing Russian attacks. Despite the constant threat of drone strikes and bombings, these dedicated individuals risk their lives daily to ensure that Ukrainians have access to electricity and heat. Since the war began, at least 160 energy workers have been killed and over 300 wounded, yet tens of thousands continue to report for duty. Workers like Mykhailo and Andrii Dzhuma express a sense of duty, understanding that their presence is crucial to prevent widespread blackouts. The attacks on the energy sector have become a strategic focus for Russia, with drones specifically targeting workers' vehicles. Despite the immense personal danger and the emotional toll, including the loss of colleagues and friends, these workers are driven by a mission to provide light and warmth. They acknowledge the risks, with some viewing their work as a form of self-sacrifice to protect civilians. The international community's provision of repair equipment is vital for their ongoing efforts. Ultimately, these workers embody the resilience of Ukraine, understanding that their commitment is fundamental to the nation's survival and the well-being of its people.
