## Article summary
Prosecutors have charged Nick Reiner, son of acclaimed director Rob Reiner, with two counts of first-degree murder in the deaths of his parents. Rob Reiner, 78, and his wife Michele Singer Reiner were found dead in their Los Angeles home with apparent stab wounds. Nick Reiner, 32, was arrested hours after the discovery and is being held on $4 million bail. The charges include special circumstances of multiple murders and the use of a knife. The district attorney's office has not yet decided whether to seek the death penalty. Rob Reiner was known for directing films like "When Harry Met Sally..." and "The Princess Bride," and was an active liberal advocate. Michele Singer Reiner was a photographer, producer, and LGBTQ+ rights advocate. The couple had been married for 36 years. Nick Reiner had previously spoken about his struggles with addiction, and he and his father collaborated on the 2016 film "Being Charlie," which explored addiction. The motive for the killings remains unknown. The deaths have sent shockwaves through Hollywood and the political community, with tributes pouring in from figures like Bill Clinton and Kathy Bates.
