## Article summary
The Trump administration has expanded its travel ban to include five additional countries: Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger, South Sudan, and Syria, also imposing new restrictions on individuals with Palestinian Authority-issued travel documents. In other news, White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles has drawn attention for candid remarks about President Trump and other administration officials in a Vanity Fair interview, though she later called the profile a "hit piece." Trump himself is suing the BBC for $10 billion, alleging defamation over the editing of his January 6th speech. Meanwhile, a federal judge appears inclined to deny a request to halt the construction of a White House ballroom, citing national security concerns. The CDC has updated its recommendation regarding the hepatitis B vaccine for newborns, suggesting it only for babies whose mothers test positive for the infection or were not tested. A former NIH scientist has filed a lawsuit against the Trump administration, claiming wrongful termination for raising concerns about research cuts. In international affairs, US-led peace efforts between Russia and Ukraine are reportedly gaining momentum, with proposals for a deal nearing finalization. The U.S. military has conducted strikes on three boats in the eastern Pacific, resulting in eight deaths, amid ongoing congressional scrutiny. Additionally, a coalition of states is suing the Trump administration over withheld funding for electric vehicle chargers.
