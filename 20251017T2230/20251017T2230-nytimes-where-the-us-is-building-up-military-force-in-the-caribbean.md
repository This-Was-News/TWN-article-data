## Article summary
The U.S. has significantly increased its military presence in the Caribbean, deploying about 10,000 troops and numerous aircraft and ships since late August. This is the largest deployment in the region in decades, aimed at countering drug trafficking and terrorism. The Trump administration has conducted lethal strikes on boats suspected of carrying narcotics, with videos of these strikes shared on social media. The buildup includes both sea and shore forces, reflecting a strategic focus on the region. The U.S. military's actions are part of a broader effort to exert pressure on Venezuela. The deployment has been analyzed through satellite imagery and military sources. The mission is framed as a counterdrug and counterterrorism operation. The increased military presence underscores the administration's commitment to regional security. The buildup has been ongoing and steady, indicating a long-term strategic approach.
