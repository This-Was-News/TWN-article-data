## Article summary
President Donald Trump announced he had commuted the sentence of former U.S. Rep. George Santos, who was serving over seven years in prison for fraud and identity theft. Santos had pleaded guilty to deceiving donors and stealing identities, including those of his family members. The S&P 500, Dow Jones, and Nasdaq saw gains, marking the best week since early August despite market volatility. Trump also discussed trade tensions with China, expressing hope for a meeting with Chinese President Xi Jinping. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy met with Trump, calling for a ceasefire and negotiations. Trump criticized the use of IVF, supported by three U.S. bishops. The Trump administration faced legal challenges over deploying the National Guard for immigration enforcement. The Supreme Court remained open but restricted public access due to the government shutdown. House Republicans refused to swear in Rep.-elect Adelita Grijalva. Trump discussed a potential deal with Ukraine for fossil fuels and criticized Hungary's purchases of Russian oil. The U.S. blocked a global fee on shipping emissions. Trump expressed optimism about more Middle East countries normalizing relations with Israel. Former Trump national security adviser John Bolton was charged with mishandling classified information.
