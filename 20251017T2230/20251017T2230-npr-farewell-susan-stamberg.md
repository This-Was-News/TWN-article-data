## Article summary
Susan Stamberg, a pioneering figure at NPR, passed away at the age of 87. She was the first woman to anchor a national evening news program, joining 'All Things Considered' in 1972. Stamberg's career at NPR spanned nearly 30 years, during which she became a legendary figure in radio journalism. She was known for her distinctive storytelling style and ability to connect with her subjects. Stamberg's influence extended beyond her on-air work, as she mentored and inspired countless colleagues. Her book, 'Every Night at Five,' chronicles her early years at NPR. Stamberg's elegance and joie de vivre were evident even in her later years. She was a role model for many women in journalism. Stamberg's legacy continues to inspire those who followed in her footsteps. She will be remembered for her significant contributions to public radio.
