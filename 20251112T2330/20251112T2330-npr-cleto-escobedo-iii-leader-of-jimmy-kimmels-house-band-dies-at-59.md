## Article summary
Cleto Escobedo III, the leader of Jimmy Kimmel's house band, has died at the age of 59. Escobedo had been a fixture on "Jimmy Kimmel Live!" since its debut in 2003. He and Kimmel were childhood friends in Las Vegas, sharing a similar sense of humor and a love for comedy. Escobedo was a musical prodigy, mastering the saxophone at a young age and earning standing ovations in junior high. His father was also a professional musician, and they were believed to be the only father-son duo performing together on late-night television. Escobedo's career included touring with Paula Abdul and performing with artists like Luis Miguel and Marc Anthony. Kimmel paid tribute to Escobedo on his show, describing him as a phenomenal musician, the nicest, most humble, kind, and funny person. He expressed the devastation felt by the show's staff and offered condolences to Escobedo's family. Kimmel concluded his tribute by urging viewers to cherish their friends.
