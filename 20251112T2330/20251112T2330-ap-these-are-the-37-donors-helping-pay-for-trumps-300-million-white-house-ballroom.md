## Article summary
The White House has released a list of 37 donors contributing to President Donald Trump's $300 million ballroom project. This diverse group includes cryptocurrency billionaires, major tech companies like Amazon and Apple, financial institutions, tobacco companies, and even some of Trump's neighbors in Florida. Notably, the list is not exhaustive, with some companies like Carrier Group and Nvidia not yet publicly listed despite their intended contributions. The White House has not disclosed the specific amounts donated by each entity, and many donors have remained tight-lipped about their contributions. Among the listed tech giants are Amazon, Apple, Google, HP, Meta, Micron Technology, Microsoft, and Palantir Technologies. The crypto sector is represented by Coinbase, Ripple, Tether, and the Winklevoss twins. Energy and industrial companies such as Caterpillar, NextEra Energy, and Union Pacific are also among the donors. Several philanthropic organizations, including the Adelson Family Foundation and the Betty Wold Johnson Foundation, have contributed. Additionally, former Trump administration officials and their families, communication and entertainment companies like Comcast and Hard Rock International, and major tobacco companies like Altria Group and Reynolds American are on the list. Defense contractors Booz Allen Hamilton and Lockheed Martin Corporation, along with several individuals like Stephen A. Schwarzman and Harold Hamm, have also made donations. No foreign individuals or entities are reported to be among the donors.
