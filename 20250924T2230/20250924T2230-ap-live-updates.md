## Article summary
A shooter opened fire at a U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) facility in Dallas, killing one detainee and injuring two others before taking his own life. President Donald Trump condemned the attack, noting the shooter wrote 'Anti-ICE' on shell casings, but did not mention the victims. The shooter was identified as 29-year-old Joshua Jahn. Trump called on Democrats to stop rhetoric against ICE and law enforcement, promising more crackdowns. Meanwhile, Trump faced criticism for unproven claims about Tylenol and autism, with figures like Hillary Clinton and Dr. Mehmet Oz weighing in. The Mexican foreign affairs ministry confirmed one injured detainee was Mexican. Trump also discussed alleged sabotage at the U.N., including issues with an escalator, teleprompter, and sound system. Additionally, Trump announced plans to sign an executive order regarding TikTok and discussed his shift in stance on the Russia-Ukraine war, expressing support for Ukraine's efforts. The shooting and its aftermath have sparked discussions on immigration, law enforcement, and political rhetoric.
