## Article summary
A devastating fire at the Le Constellation bar in Crans-Montana, Switzerland, during New Year's celebrations has resulted in 40 deaths and 119 injuries. The blaze, believed to have been ignited by candles on Champagne bottles, is one of the deadliest tragedies in Swiss history. Survivors and witnesses recount scenes of horror and heroism, with many young people rushing to help those trapped. Families are desperately searching for loved ones, with one mother looking for her 16-year-old son. Healthcare professionals describe the trauma of treating young burn victims, and volunteers recall pulling bodies from the bar. Authorities are prioritizing the identification of victims to allow families to begin mourning. Pope Leo has offered his condolences and prayers for the deceased and the injured. Officials have praised the courage and solidarity shown by citizens in the immediate aftermath of the fire. The Swiss President emphasized the nation's strength in facing such tragedies with courage and mutual support.
