Reports of explosions and fires emerged from Caracas around 2 a.m. local time, with witnesses describing blasts near military airports and other parts of the city. Videos, though not independently verified by NPR, appeared to show multiple explosions across the metropolitan area, including near the presidential palace. These events follow increased U.S. pressure on Maduro's government, which the Trump administration has accused of drug trafficking. The U.S. has recently deployed naval forces to the Caribbean, conducting strikes on vessels it claimed were transporting drugs. Maduro has previously accused Washington of seeking to control Venezuela's oil reserves.
