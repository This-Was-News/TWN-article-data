## Article summary
In the final NYC mayoral debate, frontrunner Zohran Mamdani faced attacks on his resume, while Republican Curtis Sliwa aimed to prove his seriousness. Former Gov. Andrew Cuomo, running as an independent, highlighted his political experience and defended against sexual harassment allegations. Mamdani accused his rivals of focusing on infighting rather than policy, while Cuomo and Sliwa criticized Mamdani's lack of experience and qualifications. Sliwa also mocked Cuomo's past failures. The debate touched on local issues like crime, subways, and Rikers Island, with Mamdani moderating his stance on police. All candidates opposed federal interventions in city matters. Cuomo and Mamdani clashed over handling President Trump, with Cuomo warning of Trump's potential interference if Mamdani wins. Cuomo was pressed on sexual harassment allegations, with Mamdani inviting one of Cuomo's accusers to the audience. The debate was noted for its contentious tone, with candidates also criticizing recent immigration enforcement actions.
