## Article summary
Hollywood is mourning the loss of director Rob Reiner and his wife, Michele Singer Reiner, after their son, Nick Reiner, was arrested in connection with their deaths. Los Angeles police discovered two bodies inside the couple's Brentwood home on Sunday afternoon, prompting a homicide investigation. Nick Reiner, 32, was taken into custody and his bail was set at $4 million. A spokesperson for the Reiner family expressed profound sorrow and heartbreak over the "tragic passing" and requested privacy. The Los Angeles Fire Department responded to a medical aid request before finding the bodies. Tributes have poured in from across the entertainment industry and beyond, with figures like former President Barack Obama and author Stephen King remembering Reiner's significant contributions to film and television. Norman Lear's estate recalled Reiner as a "son," while actor Harry Shearer described him as a "great collaborator" and "wonderful friend." Rob Reiner, born in 1947, had a career spanning decades, beginning with his role in the groundbreaking sitcom "All in the Family." He went on to direct iconic films such as "This Is Spinal Tap," "The Princess Bride," "When Harry Met Sally," and "A Few Good Men." His work was known for its memorable characters and quotable lines, and he was also recognized for his activism in liberal causes and social issues.
