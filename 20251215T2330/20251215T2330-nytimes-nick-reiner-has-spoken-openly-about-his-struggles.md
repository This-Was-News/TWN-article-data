## Article summary
Nick Reiner, 32, son of director Rob Reiner and Michele Singer Reiner, has openly discussed his past struggles with drug abuse and homelessness. In 2016, he shared that during his time being homeless, he could have died, attributing his survival to luck. Reiner was arrested on Sunday for the murder of his parents, Rob and Michele Reiner, who were found dead in their California home. He is being held without bail. Nick Reiner had previously collaborated with his father on the 2015 film "Being Charlie," which was loosely based on his early life experiences. The film's release marked a period of sobriety for Nick, who had struggled with addiction and homelessness since he was around 15. Rob Reiner stated that working on the film brought him and his son closer, fostering a deeper understanding of each other's experiences. Nick Reiner had expressed that the film helped him feel closer to his father, with whom he hadn't bonded much in childhood. At the time of the film's release, Nick was living back home in Los Angeles and maintaining sobriety, though he acknowledged a period of dark years. Rob Reiner had hoped to work with his son again, recognizing Nick's talent and his desire to find his own path. Nick Reiner's most recent public appearance with his father was in September at the premiere of "Spinal Tap II: The End Continues."
