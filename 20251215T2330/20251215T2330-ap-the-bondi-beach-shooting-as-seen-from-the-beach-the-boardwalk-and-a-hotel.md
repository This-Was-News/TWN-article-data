## Article summary
A shooting at a Hanukkah celebration on Sydney's Bondi Beach has deeply shaken Australia's sense of security. Two gunmen opened fire during the event, resulting in at least 15 deaths, including a child. Prime Minister Anthony Albanese has condemned the attack as antisemitic terrorism. Survivors recount harrowing experiences, hiding under tables and in the ocean as bullets replaced festive sounds. A bystander is being commended for his bravery in disarming one of the gunmen. The incident has drawn parallels to other attacks, with some survivors expressing fear and questioning the world's response to violence against Jewish people. Australian leaders have pledged to tighten gun laws in response to the massacre. The attack occurred on a typical summer evening, shattering the peace of the iconic beach. Flowers and Israeli flags now mark the site where a joyous celebration turned into a scene of tragedy.
