## Article summary
Comedy icon Dick Van Dyke recently celebrated his 100th birthday, marking a century of a prolific career that began decades ago. His enduring legacy includes iconic roles in "The Dick Van Dyke Show" and the beloved Disney classic "Mary Poppins." Van Dyke has also achieved success on Broadway, earning a Tony Award, and has been recognized with multiple Primetime Emmys and a Grammy. Notably, he became the oldest recipient of a Daytime Emmy for his guest appearance on "Days of Our Lives" at the age of 98. Throughout his life, Van Dyke has openly discussed his past struggles with alcoholism and his journey to sobriety. He recently shared his perspective on aging, noting that he often played older characters with a cantankerous demeanor, which he now finds not to be the case for himself. His book, "100 Rules for Living to 100: An Optimist's Guide to a Happy Life," offers insights into his optimistic outlook. Van Dyke credits his wife, Arlene Silver, with providing him with energy, humor, and support. He recalled his early beginnings in acting, starting at a young age and being drawn to the audience's laughter. Despite the challenges of reaching 100, including physical limitations, he continues to find joy and even attempts to dance.
