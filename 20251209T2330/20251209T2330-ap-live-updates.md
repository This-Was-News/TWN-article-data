## Article summary
President Donald Trump is holding a rally in Pennsylvania to address concerns about Americans' affordability issues, a trip that comes as public trust in his economic leadership has declined. Despite recent Republican electoral setbacks, the White House aims to convince voters that the economy will improve and that inflation is not linked to Trump's policies. Trump has blamed his predecessor, Joe Biden, for inflation, even as his own policies have contributed to price increases. The rally is taking place in a smaller venue than his typical arena events. In other news, U.S. Navy fighter jets conducted a routine training flight near Venezuela's airspace. Trump also commented on Democratic Rep. Jasmine Crockett's Senate campaign in Texas, calling her a "gift to Republicans." The Justice Department has withdrawn from an agreement with Houston to curb illegal dumping in minority neighborhoods, part of a broader rollback of environmental justice programs. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent discussed Ukraine's peace efforts and sanctions against Russia with Ukraine's Prime Minister. California Governor Gavin Newsom is writing a memoir about his life and career. International concern is growing over renewed fighting between Rwanda and Congo. Secretary of State Marco Rubio has discussed peace efforts in Sudan and the Israel-Hamas conflict with Egyptian and Saudi counterparts. A Texas congressman is running for local office instead of seeking re-election to Congress. The U.S. Supreme Court allowed a Texas congressional redistricting plan, backed by Trump, to be used in the upcoming election. Rubio also expressed concern over increased fighting between Cambodia and Thailand. In Missouri, opponents of a Trump-backed redistricting plan submitted signatures for a referendum. Ukrainian President Zelenskyy reiterated his country's refusal to cede territory to Russia, despite U.S. pressure. The U.S. labor market shows mixed signals with stable job openings but rising layoffs. A federal judge ruled that the Justice Department can release investigative materials from the Ghislaine Maxwell sex trafficking case. Trump dismissed criticism from Pope Leo XIV regarding his immigration policies. He also downplayed concerns about the impact of expiring Affordable Care Act subsidies on households. Trump gave the U.S. economy an "A-plus-plus-plus-plus-plus" rating, blaming Biden for any current economic issues. He expressed hope that Supreme Court Justices Thomas and Alito, nominated by Republican presidents, will remain on the bench. The Federal Reserve faces a contentious meeting regarding potential interest rate cuts amid economic divisions. The Supreme Court is considering a Republican-backed effort to overturn limits on political party spending. Trump stated he would allow Nvidia to sell advanced AI computer chips to approved customers in China, a decision that has drawn objections from some Democratic senators. A former Democratic Congressman announced a challenge to a Republican U.S. Representative, citing Trump's tariffs as a reason families cannot afford groceries.
