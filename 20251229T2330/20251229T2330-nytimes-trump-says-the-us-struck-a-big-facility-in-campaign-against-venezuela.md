## Article summary
President Trump announced that the U.S. struck a significant facility in Venezuela as part of an effort to disrupt drug trafficking. While officials confirmed the strike on a drug facility, they offered no specific details. Trump described the target as a "big facility" or "implementation area" where drugs were loaded onto boats. This appears to be the first known land-based attack in the U.S. campaign against Venezuelan drug trafficking. Venezuela is known for its role in trafficking cocaine from Colombia, though not as a major producer. The strike is part of an intensified pressure campaign against Venezuelan leader Nicol√°s Maduro, who faces U.S. drug trafficking charges. The U.S. has been conducting military strikes on suspected drug-carrying boats since September, which critics have labeled extrajudicial killings. These boat strikes were part of a broader operation that included plans for strikes on land facilities. The U.S. has also imposed a blockade on Venezuela, intercepting oil tankers to cut off government income. Trump has previously authorized the CIA to plan covert operations within Venezuela.
