## Article summary
The web page titled "The 100 Best Movies of the 21st Century" presents a curated list of films that have garnered acclaim from over 500 industry professionals, including directors and actors. Among the highlighted films is "Superbad," praised for its humor and heartfelt moments, and "Parasite," which defies traditional genre boundaries with its unique storytelling. Documentaries like Werner Herzog's examination of Timothy Treadwell's life and death, and "The Tree of Life," directed by Terrence Malick, explore profound themes of existence and memory. "Black Panther" stands out as a superhero film that addresses identity and responsibility while showcasing strong female characters. The list also features innovative narratives like "Everything Everywhere All at Once," which blends multiverse concepts with family dynamics. Other notable mentions include "The Hurt Locker," a gripping portrayal of modern warfare, and "Spotlight," a meticulous investigation into systemic abuse. The page encourages readers to track their viewing progress and discover films that resonate with them. Overall, it celebrates a diverse array of cinematic achievements that define the 21st century.
