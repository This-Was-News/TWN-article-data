## Article summary
The Florida Gators triumphed over the Houston Cougars to secure their third NCAA men's basketball national championship, winning 65-63 in a thrilling game held at the Alamodome in San Antonio. Despite a challenging start, where the Gators trailed by as much as 12 points, they rallied in the second half, showcasing their high-octane offense. Walter Clayton Jr. played a pivotal role, scoring crucial points after a scoreless first half, while Will Richard contributed 18 points to the team's effort. The Gators took the lead for the first time since early in the game with just 46 seconds remaining. This victory marks Florida's first national title since their back-to-back championships in 2006 and 2007. Coach Todd Golden, at 39, became the youngest coach to win an NCAA title since 1983. The Gators were one of 14 Southeastern Conference teams to compete in the tournament. Clayton was named the Most Outstanding Player of the tournament, emphasizing the team's collective effort. The Gators' scrappy defense and composure under pressure were key to their success in the championship game.
