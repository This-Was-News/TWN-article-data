## Article summary
Pete Buttigieg argues that Democrats should not aim to restore the pre-Trump status quo, as the old system was failing. He believes the party should embrace change rather than merely repairing damaged institutions. Buttigieg also discusses the Epstein files scandal, highlighting the broader issue of societal trust in government. He criticizes the "politics of fear" and advocates for a "politics of courage" to combat it. Additionally, he humorously notes the historical trend of presidential facial hair, hinting at his potential 2028 presidential run.
