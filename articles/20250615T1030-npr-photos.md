## Article summary
The "See No Kings" protests took place nationwide, with gatherings in cities from New York to Los Angeles, organized by the 50501 Movement to oppose what they perceive as authoritarian actions by the Trump administration. In Houston, protesters rallied, while thousands marched in Dallas and Austin, showcasing a strong turnout in Texas. Connecticut saw an estimated 7,000 attendees in Hartford, and similar large crowds gathered in St. Louis and Seattle. Demonstrators displayed various creative expressions, including costumes and banners, emphasizing their pro-democracy message. 

In California, significant protests unfolded in San Francisco and San Diego, where thousands participated. The protests also spread to cities like Nashville, Atlanta, and Philadelphia, with some instances of police intervention reported in Georgia. Participants expressed a sense of responsibility to protest for those unable to voice their concerns and highlighted the importance of standing against injustices. The movement even gained international attention, with protests occurring in Paris, France. Overall, the "No Kings" protests illustrated a widespread call for democracy and resistance against perceived authoritarianism across the United States and beyond.
