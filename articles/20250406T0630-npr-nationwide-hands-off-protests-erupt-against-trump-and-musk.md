## Article summary
Nationwide "Hands Off!" protests occurred on April 5, 2025, uniting various groups against the Trump administration and its policies, as well as billionaire Elon Musk's involvement in government. Over 1,300 rallies took place across the country, marking the largest protests of Trump's second term. Demonstrators voiced concerns over issues like education, healthcare, and civil rights, with many citing the administration's actions as undermining democracy and human rights. Notable protests occurred in cities such as Boston, where attendees opposed federal cuts to research and the arrest of a Tufts University student, and Portland, where thousands rallied against perceived abuses of power. 

In Washington, D.C., protesters gathered near the Washington Monument, advocating for reproductive rights, LGBTQ rights, and veterans' benefits. Veterans emphasized their commitment to protecting citizens' rights, reflecting a sense of urgency and responsibility. Many participants expressed frustration and a desire to take action against ongoing challenges to social security and other critical services. The protests served as a platform for individuals to find community and solidarity in their resistance efforts. Overall, the "Hands Off!" protests highlighted widespread discontent with the current administration and a call for preservation of democratic values and social justice.
