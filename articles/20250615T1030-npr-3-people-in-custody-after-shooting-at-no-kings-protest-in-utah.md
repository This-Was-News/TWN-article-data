## Article summary
A shooting occurred during the No Kings protest in downtown Salt Lake City, resulting in one person critically injured and two others hospitalized. The protest, attended by about 10,000 people, was generally peaceful until the gunfire erupted near 151 South State Street around 8:00 p.m. Witnesses described chaos as demonstrators began to scream and flee the area. Salt Lake City police have taken three individuals into custody as investigations continue into the shooting's motive and circumstances.

Mayor Erin Mendenhall condemned the violence, stating it is not representative of the city, while Police Chief Brian Redd praised the peaceful nature of the protest prior to the incident. Salt Lake County Mayor Jenny Wilson expressed her sadness over the gun violence, linking it to broader national issues of hate and division.
