## Article summary
The recent layoffs at the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) have created a chaotic environment, leaving employees at the CDC, FDA, and NIH in a state of confusion and uncertainty. Thousands of "reduction in force" (RIF) notices were issued starting April 1, with many employees discovering their terminations only when their security badges stopped working. Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. acknowledged that about 20% of the cuts were mistakes and announced plans to reinstate some employees and programs. However, confusion remains, as some staff members have received contradictory communications about their employment status.

The layoffs have disproportionately affected critical support roles, impacting the ability of scientists to conduct research and maintain essential public health programs. Employees have taken to crowdsourcing information about the layoffs, as the government has not provided clear details on the cuts. Many staffers expressed frustration over the abrupt and unorganized nature of the layoffs, with some fearing the long-term consequences for public health initiatives. The situation has raised concerns about transparency and communication within HHS, with the press teams also facing significant reductions.
