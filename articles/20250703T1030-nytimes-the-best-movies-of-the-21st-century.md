## Article summary
The web page titled "Readers Choose Their Top Movies of the 21st Century" presents a list of films ranked by New York Times readers, following an official list created by over 500 industry professionals. Readers cast more than 200,000 votes, resulting in a diverse selection of blockbusters and international films, including notable entries like "Sinners," "Barbie," and "Drive My Car." The list showcases a mix of genres and styles, featuring both popular hits and smaller gems, while certain titles like "Mulholland Drive" and "The Social Network" maintained strong positions. 

Readers can track their viewing progress by marking films they have seen and create their own ballots for discussion, although these won't affect the final tally. The page also includes an extensive list of the next 400 films, providing a broader scope of cinematic options. This interactive format encourages engagement and debate among movie enthusiasts. Overall, the document highlights the collective preferences of readers regarding the best films of the 21st century, reflecting a rich tapestry of storytelling in modern cinema.
