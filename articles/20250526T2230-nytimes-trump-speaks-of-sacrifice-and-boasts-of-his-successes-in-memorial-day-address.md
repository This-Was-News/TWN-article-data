## Article summary
On Memorial Day, President Trump delivered a speech at Arlington National Cemetery, honoring fallen soldiers while also addressing his political opponents and discussing his return to office. He emphasized military service and sacrifice, contrasting his remarks with previous statements made on social media that included disparaging comments about his critics. Trump also announced a plan to potentially redirect $3 billion in funding from Harvard University to vocational schools, amid ongoing tensions between his administration and the university over accusations of antisemitism. He expressed excitement for upcoming military celebrations, including a parade for the Army's 250th anniversary, which coincides with his birthday. Additionally, Trump delayed the imposition of a 50 percent tariff on European Union imports until July 9 to facilitate trade negotiations. The European Union's trade commissioner confirmed ongoing discussions with U.S. officials aimed at reaching a deal. Amidst these developments, Trump criticized both President Putin's actions in Ukraine and Ukrainian President Zelensky's rhetoric, suggesting that the ongoing war was not his responsibility. The document also highlighted Trump's contentious relationship with the military and his efforts to portray himself as a strong supporter of veterans. Overall, the day was marked by a blend of commemorative remarks and politically charged commentary.
