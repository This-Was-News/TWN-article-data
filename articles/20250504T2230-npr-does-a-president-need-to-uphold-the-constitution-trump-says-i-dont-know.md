## Article summary
In a recent interview on "Meet the Press," President Trump responded to the question of whether a president needs to uphold the Constitution with "I don't know," emphasizing that his lawyers would follow the Supreme Court's guidance. This discussion arose amidst his comments on immigration, where he expressed a desire to deport undocumented immigrants. Trump indicated that he is not seeking a third term, which the Constitution prohibits, and instead aims for four successful years in office. He also took credit for parts of the economy while blaming former President Biden for its negative aspects, describing the current economic situation as a "transition period." Trump defended his tariff policies, asserting that they would ultimately benefit the country, despite concerns about a potential recession. He expressed a willingness to consider military action regarding Greenland, citing its importance for international security, and mentioned the unlikely prospect of annexing Canada. Addressing accusations of authoritarianism, he stated that critics would have to see results to change their views. Trump affirmed that he believes critics have the right to speak out against him without fear of retribution. Overall, the interview highlighted Trump's controversial stances and ongoing political ambitions.
