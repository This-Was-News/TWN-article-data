## Article summary
Gene Hackman, the acclaimed actor known for his portrayal of tough characters, passed away at the age of 95. He had a prolific career, appearing in over 100 films and television shows, and won two Academy Awards for his roles in _The French Connection_ and _Unforgiven_. Hackman's performances embodied a gritty masculinity that defined much of the 1970s cinema. His early life was marked by challenges, including a stint in jail at 16, which he later channeled into his acting.

He often expressed a preference for playing darker roles, stating, "It's always more fun to play heavy than it is to play a good guy." Despite his tough exterior, he displayed a complex emotional range in his characters. Hackman retired from acting multiple times but always found his way back to the craft, drawn by the allure of performance. He also explored painting and writing novels in his later years. His legacy remains as one of the most respected actors of his generation, with a career that left a lasting impact on Hollywood.
