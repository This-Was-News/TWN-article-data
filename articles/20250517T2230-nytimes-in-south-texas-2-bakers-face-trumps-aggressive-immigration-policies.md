## Article summary
In South Texas, Leonardo Baez and his wife, Nora Alicia Avila, face legal challenges after federal agents raided their bakery, Abbys Bakery, detaining workers they claimed were undocumented. As their trial approaches, the couple, both legal U.S. residents, could face up to ten years in prison for harboring undocumented migrants. The case highlights the fears and divisions within the Latino-majority community of Los Fresnos, where many families are intertwined with mixed immigration statuses. Despite the legal turmoil, the Baez family continues to operate their business, which has been a cornerstone of the community for nearly 15 years. The federal crackdown on immigration under President Trump has escalated, affecting local businesses and instilling anxiety among residents. Community members express support for the Baezes, with some feeling a personal connection to their plight. Meanwhile, others emphasize the importance of adhering to immigration laws. Throughout this ordeal, Mr. Baez remains focused on his family's legacy and the bakery's future, drawing strength from his faith. The couple's story reflects the complexities of immigrant life and the pursuit of the American dream amidst challenging circumstances.
