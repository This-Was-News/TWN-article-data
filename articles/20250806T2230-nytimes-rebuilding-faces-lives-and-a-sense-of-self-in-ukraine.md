## Article summary
The article discusses the impact of facial injuries on Ukrainian soldiers and civilians due to the ongoing war with Russia. It highlights the psychological and physical toll of these injuries, which often lead to a loss of identity and societal stigma. Surgeons in Ukraine have made significant advancements in facial reconstruction, particularly using 3-D printing technology to create patient-specific implants and surgical guides. This technology allows for precise reconstruction of facial bones and features, restoring both function and appearance. The article follows the stories of several individuals, including Mr. Melnyk, a soldier who endured numerous surgeries to rebuild his face after severe injuries, and Mr. Tkachenko, a factory worker who suffered severe facial damage in a missile strike. It also mentions Ms. Leondova, who was mistaken for dead and underwent extensive reconstructive surgery. The article emphasizes the importance of international collaboration and the role of charities like Face the Future in providing advanced medical care. Despite the physical and psychological challenges, many individuals, like Mr. Poplavsky, find support in their families and medical teams. The article concludes by highlighting the resilience and determination of those affected by the war, as they strive to rebuild their lives and identities.
