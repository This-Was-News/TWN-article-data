## Article summary
Mariska Hargitay, known for her role as Olivia Benson on "Law & Order: SVU," is set to release a personal documentary titled "My Mom Jayne," which explores her late mother, actress Jayne Mansfield, and her own complex family history. Hargitay, who was only three when Mansfield died, reflects on her upbringing and the impact of her mother's celebrity and tragic death on her life. The documentary is described as both a tribute to her parents and a deep investigation into her origins, revealing that Mickey Hargitay, who raised her, is not her biological father. Through the film, Hargitay confronts family secrets and uncovers her connection with her biological father, Nelson Sardelli, leading to newfound relationships with her half-siblings. 

She emphasizes the importance of speaking truths that were previously unsaid and how this journey has brought her peace and clarity. Hargitay draws parallels between her character on SVU and her own experiences with trauma, stating that her role has allowed her to connect with survivors of abuse. Her advocacy work, including the Joyful Heart Foundation, further highlights her commitment to supporting victims. Friends and family describe her as a powerful, engaging presence who inspires others through her resilience and generosity. Ultimately, Hargitay feels empowered and ready to embrace her future, celebrating the artistic connection she forged with her mother through this documentary.
