## Article summary
The webpage showcases stunning photographs of the Strawberry Moon, which occurs in June when strawberries are fully ripe. The term "Strawberry Moon" originated from the Algonquin tribes in the northeastern U.S. and is also known as the Green Corn Moon by the Cherokee. The photos capture the moon's rise in various locations, including Halabja, Iraq; Liverpool, England; and Athens, Greece. Each image highlights the moon's beauty against iconic landmarks, such as the Empire State Building and the Parthenon. 

This year's Strawberry Moon was particularly notable, appearing approximately 14% larger and 30% brighter than usual due to its closer proximity to Earth. The event attracted many observers, as seen in images of couples and families enjoying the view at beaches and parks. The webpage emphasizes the cultural significance of the moon, linking it to strawberry harvesting traditions. Overall, the photographs celebrate a rare celestial phenomenon, enchanting viewers with the moon's luminous display across the globe.
