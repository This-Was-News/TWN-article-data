## Article summary
A recent NPR/PBS News/Marist poll reveals that nearly half of Americans (45%) give President Trump a failing grade for his first 100 days in office, with only 23% rating him positively. This reflects a decline in his approval ratings, which stand at 42%, including just 36% among independents. The poll indicates that Trump's handling of the economy and tariffs has significantly impacted public opinion, with only 39% approving of his economic management. A majority believes grocery prices will rise, and many feel that Trump's policies negatively affect them. The survey shows that 61% think Trump is moving too quickly on changes, and 85% believe he should adhere to court orders. Additionally, Elon Musk's popularity has declined, with 54% of respondents holding an unfavorable view of him. The poll highlights a general dissatisfaction with Trump's performance across various issues, including immigration and foreign policy. Overall, a significant portion of the population feels Trump has led the country in the wrong direction.
