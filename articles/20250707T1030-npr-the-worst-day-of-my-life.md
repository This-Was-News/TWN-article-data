## Article summary
Texas' Hill Country is grappling with the aftermath of devastating floods that have resulted in over 68 fatalities, including 40 adults and 28 children, with many still missing. The Guadalupe River rose dramatically, catching residents off guard, as illustrated by Melvin Harris, who described the scene as terrifying when he woke to neighbors warning him to evacuate. The floodwaters destroyed homes and infrastructure, including parts of Camp Mystic, an all-girls summer camp, where search and rescue efforts are ongoing. Texas Governor Greg Abbott has pledged that the state will continue searching for victims, with support from various agencies and volunteers. Families of the missing, like Tanya Powell searching for her daughter, are enduring immense distress as the situation unfolds. Residents, including Ryan Dale, recounted harrowing experiences as they sought safety for themselves and their children amid the chaos. The community faces a long road to recovery, with many expressing doubts about returning to their homes near the water. The floods have left a lasting impact on the area, with survivors grappling with loss and destruction.
