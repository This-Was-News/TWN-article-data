## Article summary
The flash flooding on the Guadalupe River in Central Texas has resulted in at least 51 fatalities, including 15 children, with ongoing searches for missing individuals, particularly from Camp Mystic. Julian Ryan, 27, tragically lost his life while trying to save his family as water engulfed their trailer. Sisters Blair and Brooke Harber, aged 13 and 11, also perished in the flood while staying with their grandparents, who remain unaccounted for. Katheryn Eads, 52, was swept away after escaping her RV, while 8-year-old Sarah Marsh from Alabama was confirmed among the deceased, prompting grief in her community. Janie Hunt, 9, attending Camp Mystic for the first time, was killed, leaving her family devastated. Bobby and Amanda Martin, aged 46 and 44, were camping by the river when their RV was swept away, leaving one grandchild still missing. Jane Ragsdale, the director of Heart O the Hills summer camp, was also a victim of the flooding. The community is mourning these losses, with prayer services held to honor those affected by the tragedy.
