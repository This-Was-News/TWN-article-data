## Article summary
The article discusses the impact of the Trump administration's policies on U.S. scientific research and international collaboration. In Bangalore, India, a significant number of recent Ph.D. graduates are now seeking job opportunities outside the U.S., with many opting for countries like Austria, Japan, and Australia due to tightened visa regulations and cuts to research funding. Prominent scientists express concern that the U.S. is losing its status as a leader in scientific innovation, which could have broader implications for global research and progress. The administration's actions have led to significant layoffs at institutions like Johns Hopkins University and a slowdown in grant funding from the National Science Foundation. The cultural environment in the U.S. has historically fostered groundbreaking research, and its decline is seen as a loss for the global scientific community. International governments are actively courting American scientists, indicating a shift in the global research landscape. Some researchers fear that the loss of U.S. leadership in fields like medical research and climate science will be hard to replace. The article highlights a sentiment of sadness among international students who once revered the American scientific community, now witnessing its decline. Overall, the situation presents a worrying outlook for the future of American science and its role in global collaboration.
