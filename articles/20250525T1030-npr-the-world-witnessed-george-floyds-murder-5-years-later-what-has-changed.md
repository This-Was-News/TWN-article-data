## Article summary
The article reflects on the five-year anniversary of George Floyd's murder by police in Minneapolis, which sparked widespread protests against police violence. Floyd was killed by Officer Derek Chauvin, who knelt on his neck for over nine minutes, leading to his death and the conviction of Chauvin and other involved officers. In the aftermath, significant protests occurred, resulting in over $1 billion in damages and prompting discussions about systemic racism and police reform. 

The piece explores the ongoing impact of Floyd's death through interviews with various community members, including activists, officials, and residents of the area now known as George Floyd Square, which serves as a memorial for victims of police violence. It highlights the importance of the cell phone footage that made Floyd's death a public spectacle, emphasizing the collective responsibility to confront and address such injustices. 

While some reforms and commitments have been made since the incident, the article poses the critical question of whether similar tragedies could occur again, suggesting that the issue transcends local contexts and affects society as a whole. The piece ultimately invites readers to reflect on the meaning of these events and the actions that need to be taken moving forward.
