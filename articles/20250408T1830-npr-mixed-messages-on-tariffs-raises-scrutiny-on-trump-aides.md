## Article summary
The article discusses the conflicting messages regarding tariffs from President Trump and his aides, raising scrutiny on their credibility. Trump has been promoting his efforts to negotiate tariff deals, despite his aides previously stating that tariffs were not negotiable. This inconsistency has created challenges for key officials like Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, who are tasked with explaining the administration's trade policies amid market volatility. Critics argue that the mixed messaging undermines their authority and complicates the justification for the tariffs, which are intended to address trade deficits and bring manufacturing jobs back to the U.S. 

The article highlights the tension between Trump's hardline trade advisors and those advocating for negotiation, showcasing the diverse opinions within his economic team. It notes that the chaotic rollout of tariffs has led to significant market turmoil, emphasizing the need for clearer communication. Republican strategist Alex Conant points out that the administration's rationale for tariffs appears inconsistent, complicating the sales pitch to the public and businesses. Overall, the article illustrates the difficulties faced by Trump's aides in maintaining credibility while navigating his evolving trade agenda.
