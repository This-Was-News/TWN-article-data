## Article summary
Andrew Schulz, a comedian and podcaster, has gained significant influence as a political commentator through his shows, Flagrant and Brilliant Idiots. His standup blends edgy humor with personal narratives, as seen in his recent Netflix special, "Life." Schulz's podcasts have attracted a diverse range of guests, including Donald Trump and Bernie Sanders, raising questions about his responsibilities as a media figure. He reflects on his rise to prominence, noting that he turned to online platforms when traditional networks rejected his work. Schulz emphasizes the importance of authenticity in comedy, even as he navigates the challenges of audience expectations. He discusses the perception of the "manosphere" and its appeal to young men, suggesting that simpler ideas of masculinity resonate more with this demographic. Schulz also addresses the impact of his language choices and the complexities of discussing sensitive topics in interviews. He believes that podcasting has shifted the media landscape, prompting a reevaluation of responsibilities among content creators. Ultimately, he advocates for open dialogue and diverse perspectives in political discussions. The conversation concludes with Schulz sharing a humorous family-related joke, highlighting the duality of humor and deeper feelings.
