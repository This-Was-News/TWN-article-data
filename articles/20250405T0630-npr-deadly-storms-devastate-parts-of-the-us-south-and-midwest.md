## Article summary
Deadly storms have devastated parts of the U.S. South and Midwest, with the National Weather Service warning of continued severe weather, including flooding and tornadoes. At least eight fatalities have been reported, including a young boy in Frankfurt, Kentucky, who was swept away by floodwaters. Governor Andy Beshear expressed condolences and emphasized the dangers posed by floodwaters. Other victims include a fire chief in Missouri and a man in Indiana who died from downed power lines. Tennessee has reported five additional fatalities linked to the storms. Both Kentucky and Tennessee have received federal disaster relief approval, while Arkansas has requested similar aid. The NWS forecasts that numerous rivers will reach major flood stages, with some areas expecting up to 15 inches of rain. Atmospheric conditions are contributing to the potential for intense thunderstorms and life-threatening flash flooding. The situation remains critical as communities face the aftermath of these destructive storms.
