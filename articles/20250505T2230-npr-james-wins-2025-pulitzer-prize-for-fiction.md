## Article summary
Percival Everett's novel _James_ has won the 2025 Pulitzer Prize for fiction, recognized for its powerful re-imagination of _Huckleberry Finn_ from the perspective of Jim, Huck's enslaved companion. The Pulitzer Prizes were announced on May 5, 2025, highlighting various achievements in journalism, literature, and the arts. Notable journalism awards included ProPublica's reporting on maternal health issues and The Washington Post's coverage of an assassination attempt on Donald Trump. The ceremony acknowledged the challenges faced by journalists, including financial pressures and threats to their work. Other literary awards recognized works such as _Purpose_ by Branden Jacobs-Jenkins for drama and _John Lewis: A Life_ by David Greenberg for biography. The awards celebrate excellence across multiple categories, with over 2,500 entries submitted annually.
