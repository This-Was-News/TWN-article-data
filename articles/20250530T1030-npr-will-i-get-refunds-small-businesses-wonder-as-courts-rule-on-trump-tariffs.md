## Article summary
The recent ruling by federal courts blocking President Trump's tariffs has sparked a mix of relief and uncertainty among small business owners. Many, like Sarah Wells, are hopeful about potential refunds for tariffs already paid but remain cautious as the administration plans to appeal the decision. Business owners have faced significant challenges due to fluctuating tariffs, leading some to stockpile inventory or cancel orders, as seen with Barton O'Brien's pet supply business. The legal battles have left many businesses hesitant to make new investments or shipments, creating a climate of anxiety. Rozalynn Goodwin, who sells hair barrettes, has had to renegotiate contracts and explore U.S. manufacturing options, although costs remain prohibitive. Despite temporary reductions in tariffs, many companies have raised prices to offset expenses, further impacting consumers. The uncertainty surrounding future tariffs continues to disrupt planning and financial markets, with experts noting the unpredictable nature of trade policies. Overall, small businesses are caught in a complex situation, balancing hope for refunds with the reality of ongoing tariff challenges.
