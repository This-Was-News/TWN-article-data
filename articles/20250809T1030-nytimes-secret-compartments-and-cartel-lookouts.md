## Article summary
The web page from the New York Times investigates how Mexico's most powerful criminal syndicate smuggles fentanyl into the United States. The cartel is adapting its methods in response to crackdowns by both the Mexican and U.S. governments. Reporters documented how the drug is concealed, likely using secret compartments and lookouts. The syndicate's strategies are evolving to evade law enforcement efforts. Mara Ab-Habb, an investigative correspondent based in Mexico City, reports on Latin America and authored the piece. The article highlights the challenges faced by authorities in curbing the flow of fentanyl across the border. The cartel's tactics demonstrate their resourcefulness and determination in continuing their illicit operations. The situation underscores the complex nature of combating drug trafficking between the two countries. The report provides insights into the ongoing battle against drug cartels and their sophisticated smuggling techniques.
