## Article summary
A shooter opened fire Wednesday morning during Mass at Annunciation Catholic School in Minneapolis, killing two children aged 8 and 10 and injuring 17 others, including 14 children aged 6 to 15. The shooter, identified as 23-year-old Robin Westman, used a rifle, shotgun, and pistol, firing from outside the church. All injured are expected to survive. The school, founded in 1923, had 391 students enrolled. Authorities are investigating online writings attributed to the shooter. Minnesota Governor Tim Walz expressed his condolences and ordered flags to fly at half-staff. The White House also ordered flags at half-staff until Sunday. The FBI is investigating the shooting as domestic terrorism and a hate crime targeting Catholics. The community and leaders, including the Archbishop of Saint Paul and Minneapolis, expressed shock and sadness, with many calling for an end to gun violence.
