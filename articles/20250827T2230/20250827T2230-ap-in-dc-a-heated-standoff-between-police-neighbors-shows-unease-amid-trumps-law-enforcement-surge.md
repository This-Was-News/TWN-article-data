## Article summary
A tense standoff occurred in Washington D.C.'s Mount Pleasant neighborhood between police and residents during a federal law enforcement operation. The incident disrupted the routine of the predominantly Latino community around Bancroft Elementary School, heightening existing immigration fears. Residents, including families with children heading to school, expressed outrage and fear as officers from the Washington Metro Police and ATF conducted an arrest. The operation involved a significant police presence, including plainclothes officers and tactical gear, which residents found intimidating. Aaron Goldstein, a neighborhood resident, questioned the timing and necessity of the early morning operation, which he felt terrorized children. The arrest targeted a suspect accused of drug and firearm crimes, but residents felt the operation was part of President Donald Trump's increased federal law enforcement presence in D.C. D.C. Police Chief Pamela Smith and Mayor Muriel Bowser acknowledged the anxiety caused by the operation. A Metropolitan Police Department sergeant explained that ICE officers were present to distract protesters, not to conduct immigration enforcement. Residents expressed a loss of trust in the police, while the sergeant acknowledged their frustrations. The incident highlighted the divided reactions to law enforcement in different parts of the city, with some areas appreciating the police presence while others felt threatened.
