## Article summary
The article details President Trump's lavish welcome in Saudi Arabia during his four-day Middle East visit. Upon arrival, he was greeted with military escorts and royal treatment from Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman, who showcased the opulence of the Saudi Royal Court, reminiscent of Trump's Mar-a-Lago. The president expressed camaraderie with the crown prince, appreciating the luxurious surroundings adorned with gold leaf. Trump attended the Saudi-U.S. Investment Forum, where he mingled with defense contractors and financiers, while also reflecting on the historical partnership between Saudi Arabia and the U.S. 

Prince Mohammed highlighted significant moments in their diplomatic relationship, including a desk given to Saudi Arabia's founding king by President Truman. During the visit, Trump was shown a major development project in Diriyah, aimed at transforming the historic town into a tourism hub. The president stayed at the Ritz-Carlton Riyadh, known for its past use as a detention site during a corruption crackdown led by the crown prince. The evening concluded with a state dinner, where Trump enjoyed music and a tour of the ambitious projects planned for the kingdom.
