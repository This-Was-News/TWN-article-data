## Article summary
A multilateral meeting between U.S. President Donald Trump, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy, and European leaders concluded at the White House, with hopes for a potential three-way meeting with Russian President Vladimir Putin to end Russia's war on Ukraine. The meeting discussed security guarantees for Ukraine, the annexation of Crimea, and a potential peace accord. European leaders emphasized the importance of security guarantees for Ukraine and the broader European continent. Trump expressed openness to a ceasefire and a full peace agreement, while European leaders called for pressure on Russia. The meeting was marked by a more amicable interaction between Trump and Zelenskyy compared to their previous encounter. Trump also mentioned arrangements for a meeting between Zelenskyy and Putin. Additionally, the White House was preparing for Zelenskyy's visit, with increased security and media presence. Other news included Texas Democrats ending a walkout over gerrymandered U.S. House maps and discussions on arming National Guard troops in Washington, D.C.
