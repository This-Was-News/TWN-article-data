## Article summary
George Foreman, the renowned heavyweight boxing champion, passed away at the age of 76. His family announced his death on social media, though details surrounding his passing were not disclosed. Foreman is celebrated for his notable boxing career, which included a significant loss to Muhammad Ali in the legendary "Rumble in the Jungle" and a remarkable comeback at the age of 45, when he reclaimed the heavyweight title by defeating Michael Moorer. Beyond boxing, he became a successful businessman, best known for the George Foreman Grill, which sold over 100 million units.

Foreman's life was marked by a transformation from a feared fighter to a beloved public figure, known for his humility and faith. He had a troubled youth but found purpose through boxing, eventually winning an Olympic gold medal. After retiring from boxing in 1997, he became a boxing analyst and appeared in various television roles, including a sitcom and a reality show. Foreman had 12 children, five of whom share his name, and he was remembered fondly by many in the boxing community for his generosity and friendship. His legacy as a champion, preacher, and family man will endure.
