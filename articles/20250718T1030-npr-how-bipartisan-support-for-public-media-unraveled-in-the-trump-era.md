## Article summary
The web page discusses the loss of bipartisan support for public media, particularly PBS and NPR, during the Trump era. It highlights how Congress voted to eliminate federal funding for public broadcasting, citing accusations of left-leaning bias and waste of taxpayer funds. The article traces the history of public broadcasting, noting its origins in the Public Broadcasting Act of 1967 and its initial bipartisan support. It explores the potential impact of the funding cut on local stations, especially those serving rural or tribal audiences. The piece also delves into the political and ideological controversies surrounding NPR and PBS, including criticisms of bias and the compatibility of journalism with government funding.
