## Article summary
Severe spring storms have struck the South and Midwest, resulting in a rising death toll, with at least seven fatalities reported in Tennessee, Missouri, and Indiana. The National Weather Service (NWS) has issued warnings for "potentially historic" rainfall, predicting significant risks of flash and riverine flooding across affected regions. Areas from Dallas to West Virginia are bracing for intense weather, including tornadoes and high winds. The NWS emphasized that this flooding event is expected to be prolonged, urging residents to prepare for catastrophic conditions.

Tennessee remains in a state of emergency due to the devastating impacts of the storms, while Kentucky has reported property damage and injuries. Arkansas faces threats from severe hail and tornadoes, particularly in its northeastern quarter. As the weather system continues through the weekend, communities are urged to stay vigilant and heed warnings from local authorities. The situation remains critical as millions are at risk across the impacted states.
