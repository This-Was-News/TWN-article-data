## Article summary
Federal agents conducted immigration raids in Los Angeles, sparking protests and backlash from immigrant rights groups. In response to escalating tensions, President Trump ordered the National Guard to assist federal law enforcement, claiming the need to protect ICE officers from violent protesters. California Governor Gavin Newsom criticized the federal intervention as inflammatory, stating there was no unmet need for additional resources. Protests erupted in areas like Paramount, where demonstrators clashed with law enforcement, leading to arrests. Local officials, including Mayor Karen Bass and the City Council, condemned the raids, emphasizing the contributions of immigrants to the city and vowing to support the community. The Los Angeles Police Department reported a mix of peaceful protests and unlawful assemblies, with warnings issued to disperse. Activists highlighted the raids' timing during graduation and Pride Month celebrations, framing them as cruel and unjust. Immigrant rights organizations have been preparing for such actions, educating communities on their rights and how to respond to ICE operations. Overall, the document reflects the heightened tensions surrounding immigration enforcement under the Trump administration and the community's resistance to these actions.
