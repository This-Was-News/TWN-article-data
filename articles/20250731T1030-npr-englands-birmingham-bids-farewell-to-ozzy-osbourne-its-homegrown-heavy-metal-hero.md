## Article summary
Tens of thousands of fans gathered in Birmingham, England, to pay their final respects to Ozzy Osbourne, the legendary frontman of Black Sabbath, who passed away on July 22 at the age of 76. The funeral cortege traveled through the city, pausing at the Black Sabbath Bridge, where fans had left flowers and messages. Osbourne's family, including his wife Sharon, observed the tributes and added their own bouquets. The procession was marked by a celebratory atmosphere, with fans singing along to Black Sabbath classics performed by the Boston Brass Band. Osbourne's impact on music and his hometown was celebrated, with Mayor Zafar Iqbal noting that Osbourne had "put Birmingham on the map." The event highlighted Osbourne's enduring legacy and the deep connection he maintained with his fans and the city of Birmingham.
