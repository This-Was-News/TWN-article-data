## Article summary
The article details President Trump's lavish welcome in Saudi Arabia during his four-day Middle East visit, where he received royal treatment from Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman. Upon arrival, Trump was escorted by fighter jets and greeted with grand displays, including a long lavender carpet and an honor guard. The opulent Royal Court, reminiscent of his Mar-a-Lago estate, impressed Trump, who enjoys similar luxurious aesthetics. He participated in the Saudi-U.S. Investment Forum, engaging with defense contractors and financiers amid extravagant surroundings. Trump was shown a historical desk gifted to Saudi Arabia's founding king by President Truman, highlighting the long-standing U.S.-Saudi partnership. A tour of Diriyah, a historic site being developed into a major project, showcased the Crown Prince's real estate ambitions. The evening concluded with a state dinner at the Ritz-Carlton, a hotel with a controversial past linked to the Crown Prince's power consolidation efforts. Trump's close ties to the Saudi royals were evident throughout the visit, contrasting sharply with President Biden's less favorable reception during his trip. The article reflects on the deep connections and mutual interests between Trump and Saudi leadership.
