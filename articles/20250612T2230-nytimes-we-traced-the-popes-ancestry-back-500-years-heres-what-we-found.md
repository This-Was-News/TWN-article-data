## Article summary
The web page explores the ancestry of Pope Leo XIV, revealing a complex family tree that includes noblemen, enslaved people, and slaveholders. Following the announcement of Cardinal Robert Francis Prevost as pope, genealogical research uncovered his recent African American roots, with his maternal grandparents identified as mulatto and black. The research traced his lineage back 15 generations, highlighting a diverse ancestry that includes connections to Spain, Italy, and France. Notably, some of his ancestors were involved in slavery, with four identified as slave owners, while others were enslaved. The document emphasizes the mixed-race heritage of many of his ancestors and discusses the historical context of race and identity in America, including the one-drop rule and legal definitions of Blackness. It reflects on how this ancestry encapsulates the broader narrative of American history, characterized by multiculturalism and complex heritage. The findings underscore Pope Leo's unique position as a cosmopolitan figure, embodying the diverse threads of American identity. The research was conducted in collaboration with various genealogical experts and organizations.
