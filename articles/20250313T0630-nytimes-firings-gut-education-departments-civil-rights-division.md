## Article summary
The article discusses significant staffing cuts to the U.S. Department of Education's Office for Civil Rights under President Trump's administration, which has drastically reduced its capacity to handle civil rights complaints within the education system. The firings eliminated the investigative staff in seven of the twelve regional offices, leaving thousands of pending cases unresolved. Advocates express concern, particularly for students with disabilities, fearing that the cuts will hinder the federal government's ability to enforce civil rights protections effectively. The article highlights the historical role of the office in driving policy changes and addressing complaints related to discrimination based on race, sex, and disability.
