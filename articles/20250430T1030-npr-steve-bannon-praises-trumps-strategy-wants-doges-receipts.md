## Article summary
Steve Bannon expressed strong support for Donald Trump's strategic decisions during an interview with NPR, highlighting Trump's aggressive trade policies, including high tariffs on China, as efforts to restore high-paying jobs for the working class. Bannon defended Trump's frequent shifts in trade direction, portraying them as smart negotiation tactics. He criticized other Republicans on tax and spending issues, advocating for higher taxes on the wealthy to address federal debt, a stance not fully endorsed by Trump. Bannon also called for an audit of savings claimed by Elon Musk's Department of Government Efficiency, questioning Musk's effectiveness. He referred to judicial actions against Trump as "judicial insurrection," emphasizing the need for a robust use of presidential power during challenging times. Bannon articulated a vision for America that includes revitalizing manufacturing to support traditional family structures. He believes that restoring such jobs will lead to a vibrant economy. Overall, Bannon's remarks reflect a commitment to Trump's agenda while expressing skepticism about certain allies and advocating for a return to older economic models.
