## Article summary
David Gergen, a prominent political strategist and advisor, passed away at 83. He notably shaped the public images of four U.S. presidents: Richard Nixon, Gerald Ford, Ronald Reagan, and Bill Clinton. Gergen is credited with crafting the pivotal question "Are you better off than you were four years ago?" during the 1980 presidential debate, which significantly aided Reagan's victory. Throughout his career, he transitioned from political insider to television commentator and author, sharing insights on leadership and politics. Gergen's career spanned roles in the White House, journalism, and academia, leaving a lasting impact on American political communication.
