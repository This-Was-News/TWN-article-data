## Article summary
A sergeant at Fort Stewart, Georgia, opened fire on his coworkers, injuring five soldiers, all of whom are in stable condition. The suspect, identified as Sgt. Quornelus Radford, used a personal handgun and was tackled by witnesses before being apprehended. Radford has not been previously deployed to a combat zone. Three of the victims required surgery but are expected to recover. Law enforcement responded to the scene at 10:56 a.m. and the shooter was taken into custody by 11:35 a.m. The Army installation was temporarily on lockdown. Georgia Governor Brian Kemp expressed sadness and offered prayers for the victims and their families. The incident occurred in the area of the 2nd Armored Brigade Combat Team. Army investigators have interviewed the suspect, and charges will be determined later. The families of the victims have been notified.
