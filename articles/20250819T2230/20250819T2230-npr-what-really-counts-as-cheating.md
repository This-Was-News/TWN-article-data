## Article summary
The NPR web page 'What really counts as 'cheating?': It's Been a Minute' explores the varying definitions and societal impacts of cheating in relationships. The discussion highlights that perceptions of cheating have broadened to include emotional infidelity, with many Americans considering flirting, holding hands, and forming intense emotional attachments as forms of cheating. The episode features Brittany Luse, Kathryn Jezer-Morton, and Shannon Keating, who delve into how cultural and therapeutic influences shape our understanding of love, romance, and infidelity. They also discuss the controversial idea of labeling cheating as abuse, with differing opinions on whether this classification is appropriate. The conversation touches on the negative effects of the pervasive fear of cheating in dating culture, including the lack of privacy and trust.
