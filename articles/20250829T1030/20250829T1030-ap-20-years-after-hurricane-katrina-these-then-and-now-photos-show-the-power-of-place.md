## Article summary
A web page titled 'PHOTO ESSAY: 20 years after Hurricane Katrina, these then-and-now photos show the power of place' discusses the enduring significance of physical locations where pivotal events occurred. The page features a photo essay marking the 20th anniversary of Hurricane Katrina, projecting historical images onto the same spots in New Orleans today. These images, taken by AP photographers, depict scenes of devastation and resilience, such as floodwaters, makeshift memorials, and evacuation efforts. The essay highlights how these projections evoke the chaos and fear of the past while showcasing the recovery and progress made over two decades. The photos serve as a reminder of the disaster's impact and the city's journey towards healing and moving forward.
