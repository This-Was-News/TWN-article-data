## Article summary
Pope Francis was laid to rest in a funeral Mass held in St. Peter's Square on April 26, 2025, following his death at the age of 88. The ceremony was presided over by Cardinal Giovanni Battista Re and attended by global leaders, reflecting the profound impact of Pope Francis' 12-year tenure as the head of the Roman Catholic Church. Images captured the solemnity of the occasion, showcasing the coffin being carried through crowds of mourners and dignitaries. 

In addition to the funeral Mass, the procession through Rome included a route from St. Peter's Basilica to Santa Maria Maggiore Basilica, where he was ultimately buried in a simple wooden coffin. Pope Francis, known for his humility, was the first Latin American and Jesuit pope, taking the name Francis in honor of St. Francis of Assisi. 

Across the world, people mourned his passing, with gatherings of the faithful in locations such as East Timor, where candles were lit in remembrance. The funeral emphasized both the sadness of his loss and the legacy he leaves behind, as attendees, including heads of state and clergy, paid their respects. The global response to his death highlights the significant influence Pope Francis had on millions around the world.
