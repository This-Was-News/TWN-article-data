## Article summary
"The Subtle Art of the Dad Text" celebrates the quirky and heartfelt nature of messages from fathers, particularly in honor of Father's Day. These texts often blend humor with affection, ranging from confusing emoji combinations to wise words of encouragement. In a call to readers, The Times received over 900 submissions showcasing cherished dad texts, highlighting the unique bond between fathers and their children. Many of the contributions included corny jokes, silly photos, and expressions of unconditional love, demonstrating the lighthearted yet meaningful communication style of dads. The collection captures the essence of these interactions, reflecting both the joy and the support that fathers provide through their messages. The illustrations accompanying the text add a playful touch, enhancing the overall theme of celebration. The article serves as a reminder of the warmth and humor that characterize father-child relationships through the simple act of texting. Ultimately, it honors the significant role that these communications play in our lives.
