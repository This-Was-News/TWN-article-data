## Article summary
President Xi Jinping of China is on a weeklong tour of Southeast Asia, starting in Vietnam, as he seeks to rally support against U.S. tariffs that threaten global manufacturing and economic growth. In an essay for Vietnamese media, Xi emphasized the need for stability, free trade, and cooperation, warning that there are no winners in trade wars. His visit aims to strengthen ties with Vietnam, Malaysia, and Cambodia while countering American influence. However, Vietnam must balance its relationships with both China and the U.S., as it faces significant tariffs that jeopardize its exports and foreign investments. Vietnamese officials are negotiating with Washington for lower tariffs while also trying to maintain a positive rapport with China.
