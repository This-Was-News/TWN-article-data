## Article summary
A significant storm system is impacting the Midwest and South, with violent weather including tornadoes and flash floods. The National Weather Service has warned of a "multi-day catastrophic and potentially historic" event, affecting millions from Texas to the Great Lakes. Tornadoes were reported in Arkansas, Oklahoma, Indiana, and Kentucky, resulting in at least one fatality in Missouri. Power outages affected over 250,000 customers across several states, including Arkansas, Mississippi, and Kentucky. 

Kentucky's governor declared a state of emergency as cities like Memphis and Little Rock prepared for severe conditions. The storm is expected to bring intense rainfall, with totals potentially reaching 15 inches, exacerbating flooding risks. Areas at high risk for tornadoes include parts of Arkansas, Tennessee, and Kentucky, with some tornadoes possibly classified as EF3. The NWS cautioned that the anticipated flooding could lead to significant disruptions in daily life. Overall, this event is characterized as a rare and high-impact weather situation.
