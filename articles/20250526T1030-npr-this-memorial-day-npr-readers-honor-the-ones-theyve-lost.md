## Article summary
NPR readers shared heartfelt reflections on lost loved ones in honor of Memorial Day, a holiday established to commemorate fallen military service members. The stories highlight the sacrifices and legacies of various individuals, including Capt. Hans George Faltin, who served in WWII and earned the nickname "Captain Booby Trap" for his life-saving efforts, and Spc. 5 Carl Allen Sutton, whose death from leukemia was linked to Agent Orange exposure. Kirsten Foster remembered her great-uncle, Pfc. John Francis Rys, a paratrooper killed at 18 during Operation Varsity, emphasizing the importance of keeping his memory alive. Katharine Higgins reflected on her father, Navy Lt. Theodore King Higgins, who died in a plane crash in Vietnam when she was just three. Makaela Douglas honored her grandfather, a Korean War veteran, by visiting his grave and expressing gratitude to fellow veterans. Nancy Mixson paid tribute to her parents, highlighting her father's haunting memories from liberating concentration camps and her mother's service as an Army nurse. Sheri Vandeventer's mother, a member of the Women Airforce Service Pilots, was proud of her contributions during WWII. Overall, these stories encapsulate the deep sense of honor and remembrance that defines Memorial Day for families across the U.S.
