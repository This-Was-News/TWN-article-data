## Article summary
Vance Boelter was taken into custody following a multi-day manhunt related to the shootings of Minnesota lawmakers. The FBI conducted extensive operations in Sibley County, culminating in Boelter's arrest on Sunday evening. Authorities revealed that Boelter had a list of intended targets, indicating a planned attack. Drew Evans, head of the Minnesota Bureau of Criminal Apprehension, noted that intervention by Brooklyn Park officers at the home of House DFL leader Melissa Hortman likely prevented further violence. The confrontation forced Boelter to abandon his plans for the day. The situation has raised concerns about the safety of lawmakers and the potential for targeted violence. Boelter's actions are under investigation as authorities piece together the motive and details of his intentions. This incident highlights ongoing issues related to threats against public officials. The community remains on alert following these alarming events.
