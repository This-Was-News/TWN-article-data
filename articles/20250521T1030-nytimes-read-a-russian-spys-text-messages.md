## Article summary
"Text Messages Between Russian Spies, Annotated" reveals the personal struggles of Russian deep-cover operatives Artem Shmyrev and his wife, Irina, who were assigned to Greece and Brazil, respectively. Their text exchanges, recovered from Artem's phone, highlight the loneliness and frustrations of their espionage life, showcasing imperfect English communication. Artem expresses feelings of failure and isolation, lamenting his lack of achievements and personal connections, while Irina urges him to adopt a more positive perspective and acknowledges the challenges they face. The couple's long-distance marriage complicates their situation, as they navigate the demands of espionage that often differ from their initial expectations. Irina critiques the dishonesty of their assignments, expressing dissatisfaction with the nature of their work, while Artem encourages her to document her efforts for their superiors. Their messages hint at the mundane realities of their roles, contrasting the glamorous image often associated with espionage. Ultimately, the exchanges depict two individuals coping with the weight of their dual identities and the emotional toll of their undercover assignments. The text serves as a poignant reminder of the personal sacrifices made by spies in the name of national security.
