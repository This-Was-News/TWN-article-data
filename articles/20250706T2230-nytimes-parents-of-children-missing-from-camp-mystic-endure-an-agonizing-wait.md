## Article summary
Parents of campers at Camp Mystic in Texas are enduring a painful wait for updates following catastrophic flooding that left 11 girls and one counselor missing. The camp, known for its strong community ties and historical significance, saw many parents rush to the area after receiving a brief email indicating the severity of the situation. As of Sunday morning, the search for the missing continued, with one confirmed casualty being Janie Hunt, linked to the Hunt oil family. Many campers were rescued after enduring harrowing experiences during the flood, with some recounting their terrifying nights and journeys to safety. The camp, which has been a cherished retreat for generations, features a blend of traditional activities and personal development programs. Alumni and parents remain hopeful, sharing messages of support and prayers while waiting for news. Authorities have set up a dedicated hotline for families seeking information about their missing children. The camp's director, Dick Eastland, is among those reported dead, marking a significant loss for the Mystic community. As the search continues, the bond among campers and alumni grows stronger, reflecting the deep connections formed at the camp. The tragedy has transformed a place of joy into a site of profound sorrow, leaving many grappling with the reality of the flood's devastation.
