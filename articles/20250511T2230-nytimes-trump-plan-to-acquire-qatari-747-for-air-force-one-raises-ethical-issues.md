## Article summary
Trump is reportedly planning to accept a luxury Boeing 747-8 from Qatar as a gift for use as Air Force One, which could raise significant ethical concerns regarding the intertwining of his personal business interests with his official duties. The plane, valued at approximately $400 million, would be upgraded for presidential use and later donated to Trump's library after his presidency. Critics, including Democratic lawmakers and advocacy groups, argue that this arrangement poses substantial ethical dilemmas, especially concerning the emoluments clause of the Constitution, which prohibits federal officials from accepting gifts from foreign governments without congressional approval. The proposal is still under discussion, with Qatari officials stating no decision has been made yet. The Qatari jet would offer Trump a more modern aircraft compared to his current private plane, Trump Force One. The Trump administration has faced criticism for its handling of ethical norms, particularly concerning foreign gifts and business dealings.
