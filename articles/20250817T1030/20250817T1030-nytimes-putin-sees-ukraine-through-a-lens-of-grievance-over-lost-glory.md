## Article summary
Russian President Vladimir Putin views Ukraine through a lens of grievance over Russia's lost glory since the Cold War. He demands a restoration of balance in global security, seeing the situation in Ukraine as a Western plot against Russia. Putin's rhetoric focuses on Russia's diminished status post-1991 and the need to restore its hegemony. He portrays Russia as the victim in the Ukraine conflict, which he refers to as a special military operation. Putin's stance is supported by Russian officials and media, who celebrate any perceived validation of Russia's status. The article highlights Putin's efforts to reshape the post-Cold War order and weaken trans-Atlantic relations. Despite his demands, NATO's expansion and Ukraine's resistance have complicated his goals. The article also notes the mixed reactions in Europe to U.S. President Trump's alignment with Putin's views on Ukraine. Overall, Putin's actions and rhetoric are driven by a desire to reclaim Russia's former power and influence.
