## Article summary
An NPR web page titled 'What books shaped you in high school? Here's what you said' highlights 23 influential books that readers encountered during their high school years. Over 1,100 responses were received, with 'To Kill a Mockingbird' by Harper Lee and '1984' by George Orwell being the most frequently mentioned. These books broadened perspectives, inspired careers, and fostered empathy. Other notable books include 'The Catcher in the Rye' by J.D. Salinger, 'Fahrenheit 451' by Ray Bradbury, and 'The Grapes of Wrath' by John Steinbeck. Readers shared how these books shaped their understanding of racism, authority, and societal issues. The list also includes classics like 'The Great Gatsby,' 'A Tree Grows in Brooklyn,' and 'Animal Farm,' which resonated deeply with readers. The page also suggests contemporary books like 'James' by Percival Everett, 'The Handmaid's Tale' by Margaret Atwood, and 'Night' by Elie Wiesel for today's high schoolers, emphasizing the importance of reading to broaden horizons and challenge perspectives.
