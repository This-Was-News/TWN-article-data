## Article summary
Tariffs imposed by President Trump are causing significant distress among American farmers, who largely rely on exports for their income. Farmers, like Vance Ehmke from Kansas, express that these tariffs lead to increased costs for essential supplies while lowering the prices of their products. The agricultural sector, which has already been facing economic pressures, is particularly vulnerable as tariffs threaten to diminish their competitiveness in global markets. More than 20% of farm income comes from exports, and retaliatory tariffs are expected to further reduce demand for U.S. agricultural products.

Trade associations warn that losing market share can have long-term consequences, as building trading relationships takes decades. Farmers are concerned that countries affected by U.S. tariffs may turn to competitors like Brazil and Argentina for their needs.
