## Article summary
Peru has embraced Pope Leo XIV, who served as the bishop of Chiclayo from 2015 to 2023, as one of their own despite being born in Chicago. Celebrations in Chiclayo included applause from passengers on a flight and public gatherings where people sang praises and displayed posters of the pope. Pope Leo XIV's commitment to Peru began in 1985, during a tumultuous period marked by internal conflict, and he later returned to the country to become a citizen as required by a diplomatic treaty with the Vatican. Known for his warmth and hands-on approach, he helped communities affected by floods and engaged deeply with local culture, expressing fondness for regional cuisine. His first public address as pope included a heartfelt mention of Chiclayo, highlighting his strong ties to the community. Many locals, including church volunteers and priests, praised his humility, patience, and dedication to helping the poor. They noted that he represented a continuity of compassion similar to that of Pope Francis. As the excitement in Chiclayo grew, residents reflected on his legacy and their hopes for his future, joking about his potential to become a saint. Overall, the city celebrated Pope Leo XIV's dual identity, bridging his origins in Chicago with his profound impact in Peru.
