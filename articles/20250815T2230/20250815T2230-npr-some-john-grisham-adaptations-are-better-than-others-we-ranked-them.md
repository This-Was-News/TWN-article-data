## Article summary
The article ranks John Grisham's adaptations from film and television, noting that while his stories often follow a similar theme of an underdog fighting against powerful entities, the quality of adaptations varies. The new TV series 'The Rainmaker' is compared to other adaptations like 'The Firm,' 'The Chamber,' and 'A Time to Kill,' with 'The Pelican Brief' and the 1993 'The Firm' receiving the highest scores for capturing Grisham's essence. The rankings consider factors like plot, cast, and how closely the adaptation aligns with Grisham's typical themes and style. The article highlights notable performances and scenes while critiquing some adaptations for deviating too much from the source material or failing to capture the propulsive nature of Grisham's stories.
