## Article summary
The New York Times article discusses how President Trump's hardline immigration policies are significantly reducing migration at the U.S.-Mexico border. Illegal crossings have dropped to their lowest levels in decades, with many migrants returning home instead of heading north. Building on the restrictions initiated by President Biden, Trump has implemented measures such as halting asylum indefinitely, deploying military forces, and pressuring Latin American governments to curb migration.

Statistics show a steep decline in migrant apprehensions, with only 8,347 apprehensions reported in February 2025, a stark contrast to over 225,000 in December 2023.
