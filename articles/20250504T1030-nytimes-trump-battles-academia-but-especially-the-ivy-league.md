## Article summary
The article discusses President Trump's contentious relationship with Ivy League universities, particularly Harvard, highlighting his criticism of these elite institutions as hubs of liberal indoctrination and antisemitism. Trump's rhetoric has resonated with his political base, leading him to threaten federal funding cuts to several Ivy League schools. Despite being an Ivy League graduate himself, Trump feels rejected by this academic elite, which has fueled his attacks on them. His administration has targeted numerous universities for perceived antisemitism, with a particular focus on Harvard and the University of Pennsylvania, his alma mater. Trump's complicated history with Penn includes never having received an honorary degree or delivered a commencement address there. He has also been known to exaggerate his academic achievements, claiming to be at the top of his class despite evidence to the contrary. Although his cabinet includes some Ivy League graduates, Trump has shown a tendency to disparage elite academia while simultaneously highlighting the credentials of certain appointees. Overall, the article portrays Trump's ongoing conflict with the Ivy League as rooted in both resentment and a desire for acceptance.
