## Article summary
The article lists the 100 best movies of the 21st century, as voted by over 500 influential figures in the film industry. It highlights a diverse range of films, from comedies like "Superbad" to intense dramas such as "12 Years a Slave." The list emphasizes innovative storytelling, groundbreaking performances, and unique visual styles, showcasing films like "Gravity," which explores isolation in space, and "Moonlight," which delicately portrays the life of a Black gay man. Notable mentions include "Get Out," which critiques societal issues through horror, and "Pan's Labyrinth," a dark fairy tale set against the backdrop of fascist Spain. The document also covers films that push the boundaries of genre, such as "Inception," which merges action with philosophical questions about reality. Directors like Quentin Tarantino and Martin Scorsese are recognized for their contributions to modern cinema, with films like "Once Upon a Time in Hollywood" and "The Irishman." The list reflects the evolution of storytelling in film, emphasizing emotional depth and cultural relevance. Each film is noted for its ability to resonate with audiences, whether through humor, tragedy, or thought-provoking themes.
