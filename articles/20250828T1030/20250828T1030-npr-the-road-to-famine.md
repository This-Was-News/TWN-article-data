## Article summary
The article discusses the U.S. policy failures in addressing the humanitarian crisis in Gaza, highlighting the struggles to deliver adequate food and aid to Palestinians amid Israel's blockade. Key points include the U.S. administration's internal debates, the ineffectiveness of air drops and a floating pier, and the eventual declaration of famine in Gaza. The piece also explores the political and diplomatic challenges faced by U.S. officials, including President Biden's personal commitment to Israel and the administration's reluctance to publicly criticize Israel's actions. The article concludes by noting the worsening humanitarian situation under the Trump administration and the ongoing debate over U.S. policy and its impact on Gaza.
