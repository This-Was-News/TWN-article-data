## Article summary
Vice President JD Vance's family boating trip in Ohio required the Secret Service to raise river levels for security, which sparked criticism. Trump also signed an executive order aimed at preventing banks from closing accounts for political or religious reasons. The Education Department launched an investigation into Baltimore County Public Schools over allegations of antisemitism. Trump ordered the Commerce Department to begin work on a new census excluding undocumented immigrants, facing legal challenges. The F.B.I. under Trump-appointed leadership forced out several senior agents, including those involved in politically sensitive cases. Senator John Cornyn requested F.B.I. assistance to locate Texas Democratic lawmakers who fled the state to block redistricting. Trump expressed willingness to meet with Russian President Vladimir Putin, with potential implications for the Ukraine conflict. The White House pushed for a cease-fire in Ukraine, but Russia's stance remained unclear. Trump's actions and policies continue to draw both support and criticism, with significant implications for various sectors and institutions.
