## Article summary
The Kentucky Derby highlights the significant role of Latinos in the horseracing industry, showcasing their contributions from grooms to successful jockeys. Many of these jockeys, such as Junior Alvarado from Venezuela, have pursued their dreams in the U.S. to compete in this prestigious race, often facing challenges related to immigration status. The presence of Latino jockeys has increased over the years, with estimates suggesting they make up a substantial portion of competitors in American racing. 

Jockeys from countries like Mexico, Panama, and Venezuela often come to the U.S. on athlete visas, while others are U.S.-born, including those from Puerto Rico. The industry recognizes the sacrifices these athletes make, as they leave families behind and train extensively to succeed. Despite potential regulatory changes affecting immigration, many jockeys continue to thrive, underscoring the importance of foreign talent in maintaining the excitement of the Derby. 

Organizations like the Jockeys' Guild work to assist these athletes with their immigration processes. The contributions of Latino jockeys have been celebrated, as they are considered essential to the Derby's legacy and success. Ultimately, the Kentucky Derby stands as a testament to the excellence and dedication of Latino athletes in the sport.
