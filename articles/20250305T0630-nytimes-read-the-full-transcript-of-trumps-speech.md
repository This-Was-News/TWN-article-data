## Article summary
In his speech to Congress, President Trump outlined his ambitious agenda for remaking U.S. policy across various sectors, including military, trade, immigration, and foreign aid, emphasizing swift action taken within his first weeks in office. He claimed significant achievements, such as signing nearly 100 executive orders and declaring a national emergency on the southern border, which he credited for reducing illegal border crossings. Trump criticized the previous administration's policies, particularly regarding immigration and economic management, asserting that his administration is focused on restoring law and order, supporting law enforcement, and combating crime. 

He announced plans to eliminate wasteful government spending and fraud, highlighting the creation of a new Department of Government Efficiency. The president also proposed tax cuts aimed at providing relief to working families and boosting domestic production. Trump reiterated his commitment to protecting American values, including banning transgender ideology in schools and supporting traditional gender identities. He addressed national security, stating that the U.S. would take a stronger stance against cartels and illegal immigration, while also pursuing peace in international conflicts such as the war in Ukraine. In conclusion, Trump expressed optimism about America's future, proclaiming it as the start of a "golden age" for the nation.
