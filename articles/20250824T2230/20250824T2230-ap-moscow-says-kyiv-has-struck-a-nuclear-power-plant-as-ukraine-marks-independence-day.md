## Article summary
Russia accused Ukraine of launching drone attacks that sparked a fire at a nuclear power plant in the Kursk region overnight, as Ukraine celebrated its 34th Independence Day. Russian officials reported several power and energy facilities were targeted, with the fire at the nuclear plant quickly extinguished and no injuries reported. The United Nations nuclear watchdog acknowledged media reports of the incident but had not received independent confirmation. Ukraine did not immediately comment on the alleged attack. Meanwhile, firefighters responded to a blaze at the port of Ust-Luga in Russia's Leningrad region, with the governor reporting that approximately 10 Ukrainian drones were shot down. Russia's Defense Ministry claimed its air defenses intercepted 95 Ukrainian drones overnight into Sunday. Ukraine's air force reported that Russia fired 72 drones and decoys, along with a cruise missile, into Ukraine overnight into Sunday, with 48 drones shot down or jammed. The incidents occurred as Ukraine marked Independence Day, with President Volodymyr Zelenskyy delivering a video address emphasizing the nation's resolve. Zelenskyy called for a just peace and highlighted Ukraine's strength and power. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney arrived in Kyiv for meetings with Zelenskyy, with Ukraine's chief of staff expressing gratitude for Canada's support. Norway announced significant new military aid, pledging about $695 million for air defense systems. Meanwhile, fighting continued on the front line in eastern Ukraine, with Russia claiming to have seized two villages in the Donetsk region.
