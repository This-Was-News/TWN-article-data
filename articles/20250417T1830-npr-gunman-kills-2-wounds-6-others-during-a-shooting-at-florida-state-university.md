## Article summary
A tragic shooting at Florida State University (FSU) resulted in the deaths of two individuals and injuries to six others. The suspect, identified as Phoenix Ikner, a 20-year-old FSU student and son of a Leon County sheriff's deputy, is currently hospitalized. The incident occurred at the Student Union, prompting authorities to issue a shelter-in-place alert. Law enforcement neutralized the threat shortly after the shooting, but several buildings remained crime scenes. The gunman had access to a personal handgun belonging to his father, which was found at the scene alongside a shotgun. 

FSU has canceled classes and all athletic events through the weekend as the community grapples with the aftermath. Governor Ron DeSantis and President Trump expressed their condolences and support. Local healthcare facilities reported one critical and five serious injuries among the victims. In response to the shooting, the FSU College Democrats labeled it a preventable act of violence, emphasizing the need for safety on campus. This incident echoes a previous shooting at FSU in 2014, highlighting ongoing concerns about campus safety.
