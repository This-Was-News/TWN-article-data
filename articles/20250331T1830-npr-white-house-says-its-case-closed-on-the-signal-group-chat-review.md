## Article summary
The White House has concluded its review of the incident involving the Signal group chat, where Atlantic editor Jeffrey Goldberg was inadvertently included in discussions among high-ranking officials about military actions in Yemen. Press secretary Karoline Leavitt announced that the case is considered "closed" and that steps are being taken to prevent similar occurrences in the future. She did not provide specific details about these measures. Previously, the National Security Council, the White House counsel's office, and Trump adviser Elon Musk were involved in investigating the mishap. Leavitt emphasized that the information shared in the chat was not classified. The incident raised concerns among security experts due to its sensitive nature. National security adviser Mike Waltz, who created the group chat, remains a key member of Trump's national security team. The White House has indicated a commitment to moving forward after this review.
