## Article summary
The summit between U.S. President Donald Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin in Alaska on August 15, 2025, was marked by high expectations but yielded no clear results or major announcements. Despite elaborate staging, the diplomatic script seemed unprepared, and Trump's brief remarks indicated dissatisfaction. Trump mentioned some agreed points but did not elaborate on details. Global reactions were mixed, with hope and disappointment. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy, excluded from the talks, planned to meet Trump to discuss ending the war. U.S. legislators expressed criticism and cautious optimism, with some urging stronger sanctions on Russia. Trump postponed sanctions despite Putin's lack of commitment to a ceasefire. European leaders, who had supported Ukraine, expressed anger and concern over Putin's influence and continued Russian attacks. The summit highlighted the complex dynamics and challenges in achieving peace in Ukraine.
