## Article summary
Israeli strikes on Nasser Hospital in Khan Younis, Gaza, killed 22 people, including five journalists, drawing global condemnation. The journalists worked for international outlets like The Associated Press, Reuters, and Al Jazeera. The strikes targeted the hospital's fourth-floor stairwell, where journalists and medical staff were present. Witnesses reported two consecutive strikes, with the second hitting as rescuers and journalists responded to the first. Among the victims was Maram Dagga, a freelance journalist who contributed to The Associated Press, highlighting the dire conditions in Gaza. The attack marked one of the deadliest days for journalists in the Palestinian territory, with 245 media workers killed in the past 22 months. Journalists and media organizations condemned the attack, demanding a transparent investigation and questioning Israel's targeting of live feeds. The Israeli military acknowledged the attack but did not identify a specific target, expressing regret for any harm to civilians. The incident has intensified calls for accountability and an end to the targeting of journalists.
