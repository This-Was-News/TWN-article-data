## Article summary
Pope Francis was laid to rest on April 26, 2025, following a funeral Mass held in St. Peter's Square at the Vatican, attended by global leaders and dignitaries. He passed away at the age of 88 after serving as the head of the Roman Catholic Church for 12 years. The funeral Mass was led by Cardinal Giovanni Battista Re, the dean of the College of Cardinals. Throughout the ceremony, images captured the profound grief and respect from attendees, including clergy and the faithful.

Following the Mass, the coffin was transported through Rome, passing significant landmarks like the Colosseum, before being taken to Santa Maria Maggiore Basilica for burial. Pope Francis, the first Latin American and Jesuit Pope, was known for his humility and dedication to the Church. His final resting place is marked by a simple wooden coffin, reflecting his modest approach to leadership.

Around the world, people mourned his loss, with gatherings and vigils held in various locations, including East Timor, where believers held candles in his memory. The event underscored the Pope's impact on millions globally and the legacy he leaves behind.
