## Article summary
Late-night hosts Jon Stewart, Stephen Colbert, and Jimmy Fallon addressed the suspension of Jimmy Kimmel on their shows with a mix of humor and solidarity. Stewart used satire to critique ABC's decision, Colbert called it blatant censorship, and Fallon praised Kimmel while humorously highlighting censorship. Their guests varied, with Stewart and Colbert interviewing those who could address censorship concerns. Maria Ressa, a journalist and Nobel Peace Prize recipient, shared her experiences with censorship under former Philippine President Rodrigo Duterte. Stewart's show featured a satirical take on adherence to the party line, mocking President Donald Trump's policies and decor. Fallon opened his monologue by expressing support for Kimmel, hoping he would return. Colbert also stood with Kimmel, criticizing ABC's decision and defending freedom of speech. David Letterman, Colbert's predecessor, lamented the network's moves, calling it managed media and criticizing the firing of those who don't support an authoritarian president.
