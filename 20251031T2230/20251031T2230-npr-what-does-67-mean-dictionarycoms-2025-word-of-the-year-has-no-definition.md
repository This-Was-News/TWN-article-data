## Article summary
Dictionary.com has named "67" as its 2025 word of the year, a slang term that, notably, has no concrete definition. The term originated from a song by Philadelphia rapper Skrilla and gained widespread popularity through viral TikTok videos, particularly those featuring basketball highlights. NBA players like LaMelo Ball, LeBron James, and Paige Bueckers have used the term, further amplifying its reach across sports and into schools. "67" is typically delivered with a slow drawl and a specific hand gesture, often used by children to reference the number 67 in various contexts. Searches for "67" on Dictionary.com surged significantly over the summer, indicating its rapid spread. The word's success highlights the speed at which new language can travel globally, especially among younger generations. Despite its lack of definition, "67" has fostered a sense of connection and belonging, acting as a shared inside joke for its users. The rapper Skrilla and basketball player LaMelo Ball have both acknowledged the term's intentionally vague nature. Parenting experts suggest that "67" serves as a valuable connection point between adults and adolescents, enabling meaningful conversations. The term's ubiquity and nonsensical nature are seen as a reflection of the perpetually online culture.
