## Article summary
California Governor Gavin Newsom has ordered flags in Sacramento to be flown at half-staff in honor of U.S. Representative Doug LaMalfa, a Republican who represented Northern California's 1st Congressional District for over 16 years. LaMalfa passed away suddenly at the age of 65 on January 5, 2026, during emergency surgery in Chico. The Butte County Sheriff's Office is investigating the cause of death. LaMalfa was remembered by colleagues from both parties as a devoted public servant who fiercely advocated for his constituents and the American values he cherished. Governor Newsom described him as a devoted public servant who deeply loved his country, state, and communities. Assembly Minority Leader James Gallagher recalled LaMalfa as a mentor and a dedicated public servant whose focus was always on his district. U.S. Representative Young Kim highlighted his integrity, conviction, and commitment to working families, farmers, and small businesses. Los Angeles Mayor Karen Bass remembered him as an upstanding colleague and a fierce advocate, noting their ability to collaborate despite differing perspectives. U.S. Representative John Garamendi, who served with LaMalfa for nearly fifteen years, spoke of their deep friendship and shared work on bipartisan bills for water infrastructure and California farmers. LaMalfa's dedication to his district and his bipartisan approach were widely acknowledged by those who knew him.
