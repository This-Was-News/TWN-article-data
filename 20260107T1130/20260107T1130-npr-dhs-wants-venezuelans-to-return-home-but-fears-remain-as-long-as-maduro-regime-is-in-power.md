## Article summary
The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) is urging Venezuelans in the U.S. to return home, despite ongoing fears about the stability of the Maduro regime. This push comes after the Trump administration ended Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for over 600,000 Venezuelans last year, citing improved conditions in Venezuela. However, many Venezuelans, like Sebastian, a 28-year-old architect, express disbelief, pointing to the U.S. government's recent actions to remove President Maduro as evidence that the situation remains dangerous. The State Department advises U.S. citizens against travel to Venezuela due to risks including wrongful detention and torture. Reports of journalists being detained and armed groups suppressing dissent further fuel these concerns.
