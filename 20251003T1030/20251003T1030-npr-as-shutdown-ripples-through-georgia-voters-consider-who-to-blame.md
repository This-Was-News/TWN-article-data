## Article summary
The federal government shutdown has entered its third day, impacting national parks, federal employees, and various sectors in Georgia. The Atlanta Community Food Bank is preparing for increased demand, while the CDC faces additional strain due to furloughed employees and potential layoffs. Georgia farmers worry about delays in funding and the Farm Bill. The Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area is affected by reduced staff and canceled field trips. Voters in Georgia have mixed opinions on who is to blame for the shutdown, with some focusing on the impact on federal workers and others on political stances. The shutdown's duration and its effects on health insurance subsidies and federal programs remain significant concerns.
