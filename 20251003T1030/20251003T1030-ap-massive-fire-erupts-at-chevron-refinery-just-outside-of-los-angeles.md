## Article summary
A fire broke out at the Chevron oil refinery near Los Angeles on Thursday night, with flames visible for miles. Officials urged residents to stay indoors, and by early Friday, the fire was contained with no public safety threat. No evacuations were ordered, but road closures remained. Residents reported feeling a rumble and seeing an orange sky. There were no injuries, and all personnel were accounted for. The cause of the fire was not disclosed. Fire crews contained the blaze to one section of the refinery. California Governor Gavin Newsom's office monitored the situation. The refinery, operational since 1911, can refine up to 290,000 barrels of crude oil per day. No immediate air pollution issues were detected.
