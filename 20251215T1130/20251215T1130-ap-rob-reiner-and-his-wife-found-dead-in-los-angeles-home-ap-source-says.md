## Article summary
Director-actor Rob Reiner, 78, and his wife Michele Singer Reiner, 68, were found dead in their Los Angeles home on Sunday afternoon. Authorities are investigating the incident as an apparent homicide, with preliminary findings suggesting stab wounds. A family member is reportedly being questioned by investigators. Reiner, known for directing films like "When Harry Met Sally" and "The Princess Bride," as well as his Emmy-winning role as Meathead on "All in the Family," had a significant impact on American culture. His contributions extended beyond his creative work to activism and philanthropy. The Los Angeles Police Department confirmed detectives from the Robbery Homicide Division are investigating. The couple, married since 1989, had three children. The incident occurred in the upscale Brentwood neighborhood, an area not typically associated with violent crime. The Los Angeles Fire Department responded to a medical aid request before discovering the bodies. Reiner's death is considered a "devastating loss" by Los Angeles Mayor Karen Bass, who highlighted his work for social and economic justice. The son of comedy legend Carl Reiner, he was previously married to Penny Marshall.
