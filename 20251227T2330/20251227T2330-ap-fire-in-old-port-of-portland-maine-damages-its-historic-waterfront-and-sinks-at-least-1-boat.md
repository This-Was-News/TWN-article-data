## Article summary
A fire severely damaged Portland, Maine's historic Old Port waterfront on the day after Christmas. The blaze spread through aging buildings along Custom House Wharf, a significant area for the city's fishing industry and now home to seafood restaurants. Firefighters utilized a fire boat to combat the flames due to issues with frozen hydrants. Several boats were impacted, with at least one sinking. The cause of the fire remains under investigation, and one firefighter sustained minor injuries. The Porthole Restaurant, located on the wharf, confirmed that its staff, fishermen, and owners were safe. The incident occurred on Friday, December 27, 2025.
