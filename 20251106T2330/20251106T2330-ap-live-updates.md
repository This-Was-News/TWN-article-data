## Article summary
A federal judge in Rhode Island has ordered the Trump administration to fully fund SNAP benefits for November, overriding the administration's plan to provide only partial funding due to the government shutdown. The judge criticized the administration for failing to consider the practical consequences and harm to individuals relying on these benefits. In other news, the FAA is reducing air traffic by 10% at 40 major airports due to the government shutdown, leading to flight cancellations. The Supreme Court has allowed the Trump administration to enforce its policy blocking transgender and nonbinary people from choosing their preferred sex markers on passports. President Trump has unveiled a deal with drug manufacturers to expand coverage and lower prices for popular obesity drugs, Zepbound and Wegovy. Meanwhile, the government shutdown has reached 37 days, making it the longest in U.S. history. Separately, a judge in Chicago will order federal agents to restrict the use of force against protesters and media, citing constitutional rights violations. Kazakhstan is set to join the Abraham Accords with Israel, a symbolic move to boost the initiative. Finally, Nancy Pelosi announced she will not seek reelection, ending her significant career in the U.S. House of Representatives.
