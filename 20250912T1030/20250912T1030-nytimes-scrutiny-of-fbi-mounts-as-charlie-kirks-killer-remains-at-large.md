## Article summary
F.B.I. Director Kash Patel and his deputy, Dan Bongino, are under intense scrutiny following a series of missteps and the ongoing manhunt for the killer of Charles Kirk. Patel convened an online meeting with 200 agents, expressing fierce urgency and pressure to catch the killer. He criticized subordinates for failing to provide timely information, including a 12-hour delay in showing him a photo of the suspected killer in Salt Lake City. The situation highlights concerns about Patel's leadership and the F.B.I.'s ability to maintain public confidence.
