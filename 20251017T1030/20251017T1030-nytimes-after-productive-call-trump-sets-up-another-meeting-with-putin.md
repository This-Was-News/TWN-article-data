## Article summary
President Trump plans to meet with Russian President Vladimir Putin in Hungary to discuss ending the war in Ukraine. After a two-hour call with Putin, Trump expressed plans to meet with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky, who is expected to request advanced weapons for Ukraine. Trump has previously considered providing Tomahawk missiles to Ukraine but has expressed hesitation, influenced by Putin's opposition. Trump's approach to the conflict has been marked by repeated threats of penalties against Russia, followed by diplomatic engagement with Putin. Critics argue that Trump is rewarding Putin without securing concessions. Despite past failures, Trump remains optimistic about his ability to broker peace, citing his success in the Middle East. The upcoming meeting between Trump and Putin is uncertain, with experts cautioning that it depends on preliminary talks between senior officials. Trump has expressed frustration with Putin's continued attacks on Ukraine but struck a cordial tone after their recent call. Putin congratulated Trump on his Middle East diplomatic efforts and expressed gratitude for Melania Trump's role in securing the return of Ukrainian children. The administration faces criticism for potentially repeating past mistakes in dealing with Putin.
