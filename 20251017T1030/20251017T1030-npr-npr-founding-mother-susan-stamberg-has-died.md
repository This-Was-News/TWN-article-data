## Article summary
Susan Stamberg, a pioneering figure at National Public Radio (NPR), has passed away at the age of 87. She was the first U.S. woman to anchor a nightly national news program and was known for her distinctive voice and engaging storytelling. Stamberg began her career at NPR before its broadcast debut, initially assigned to cut audio tape. She quickly rose to prominence, becoming the anchor of *All Things Considered* in 1972. Throughout her career, Stamberg covered a wide range of topics, from political interviews to cultural segments, and was known for her unique approach to journalism. She launched the Sunday puzzle on *Weekend Edition Sunday* and brought the popular show *Car Talk* to NPR. Stamberg's contributions to NPR were recognized with numerous awards, including induction into the National Radio Hall of Fame and a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. She retired in September 2023, leaving behind a legacy as a mentor and a founding mother of NPR. Stamberg is survived by her son Josh and granddaughters Vivian and Lena.
