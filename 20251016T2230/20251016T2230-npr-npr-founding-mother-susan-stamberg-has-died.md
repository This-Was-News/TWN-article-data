## Article summary
Susan Stamberg, a pioneering figure at National Public Radio (NPR), passed away at the age of 87. She was the first U.S. woman to anchor a nightly national news program and was known for her distinctive voice and engaging storytelling. Stamberg began her career at NPR before its official broadcast debut, initially cutting audio tape and later becoming a producer and anchor. She was recognized for her unique approach to journalism, blending news with cultural insights and human-interest stories. Stamberg's segments often featured interviews with notable figures and explored a wide range of topics, from politics to arts. She was honored with numerous awards, including induction into the National Radio Hall of Fame and a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. Stamberg's legacy includes the creation of NPR's Sunday puzzle and the launch of the popular show Car Talk. She retired in September 2023, leaving behind a lasting impact on public radio.
