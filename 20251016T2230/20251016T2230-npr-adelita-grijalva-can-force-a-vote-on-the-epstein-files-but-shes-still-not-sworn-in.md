## Article summary
Rep.-elect Adelita Grijalva, a Democrat from Arizona, has faced delays in being sworn into Congress due to partisan tensions and a government shutdown. Despite winning her seat over three weeks ago, she has not been sworn in, leaving her without staff, a working phone, or a government email. House Speaker Mike Johnson has refused to swear her in until the shutdown ends, a departure from his previous practice of swearing in new members quickly. Grijalva's supporters and Democrats accuse Johnson of delaying her swearing-in to avoid a vote on releasing the Epstein files, which she promised to support. Johnson denies these accusations, stating that Republicans are already investigating the matter.
