## Article summary
Despite a challenging year for the film industry, characterized by declining box office numbers and the looming threat of AI, film critic Justin Chang highlights ten exceptional movies released in 2025. The top film, 'SirƒÅt,' is a Spanish survival thriller set in Morocco, praised for its exhilarating and devastating impact. 'One Battle After Another,' directed by Paul Thomas Anderson, is a vibrant blend of action and political satire featuring a standout performance by Leonardo DiCaprio. 'Caught by the Tides' by Jia Zhangke offers a unique fusion of fiction and nonfiction, exploring China's transformations. 'Resurrection,' another Chinese film, is a dreamlike odyssey through cinematic genres. The documentary 'My Undesirable Friends: Part I Last Air in Moscow' captures the struggles of Russian independent journalists. 'The Secret Agent' is a moving drama set during Brazil's military dictatorship. 'Sound of Falling' is a German drama described as the year's best haunted-house movie. 'April' presents a stark yet hypnotic look at healthcare provision in rural Georgia. 'On Becoming a Guinea Fowl' is a Zambian film exploring dark family secrets. Finally, 'It Was Just an Accident,' winner of the Palme d'Or, is a powerful moral thriller from Iranian director Jafar Panahi.
