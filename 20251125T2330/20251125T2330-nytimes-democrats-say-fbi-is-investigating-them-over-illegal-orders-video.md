## Article summary
A coalition of educators and advocacy groups have filed a lawsuit against the Trump administration, challenging the legality of dismantling the Education Department. The lawsuit argues that the Education Secretary lacks the authority to transfer the department's functions to other agencies. Meanwhile, Representative Eric Swalwell has sued the director of the Federal Housing Finance Agency, alleging privacy law violations and the misuse of federal mortgage documents to pursue political opponents. A small group of Republicans in Congress, facing re-election challenges, are advocating for a temporary extension of Affordable Care Act subsidies, breaking with their party's stance. Democrats, including veterans, claim the FBI is investigating them for recording a video advising troops to refuse illegal orders, asserting this is a tactic by President Trump to intimidate political adversaries. The FBI has declined to comment on the investigation. In other news, President Trump has pardoned a turkey, a Thanksgiving tradition. The Army secretary met with Russian envoys in the UAE to discuss President Trump's peace plan for Ukraine, with the US official stating Ukraine is on board with the plan. President Trump also signed an executive order to build an AI platform using federal scientific data for research and development. Additionally, President Trump took a step toward designating parts of the Muslim Brotherhood as foreign terror organizations, which could lead to travel and economic restrictions. A former Justice Department lawyer, who spoke out against the administration's deportation policies, has joined a legal group challenging the Trump administration's approaches to immigration and civil rights. Finally, a group of federal workers laid off during the government shutdown are accusing the administration of violating the law by not rehiring them, despite a spending law provision intended to reverse such layoffs.
