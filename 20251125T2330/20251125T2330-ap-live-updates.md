## Article summary
The FBI has contacted Democratic lawmakers who appeared in a video urging U.S. troops to defy illegal orders, signaling a potential inquiry. This follows a Pentagon investigation into Senator Mark Kelly and President Trump's accusations of sedition. In other news, the Trump administration plans to review refugees admitted under the Biden administration, sparking concerns among advocates. Washington Mayor Muriel Bowser announced she will not seek reelection after a tumultuous term. Indiana lawmakers are set to decide on redistricting in December, with Trump expressing hope for the Senate to find the votes. The FEMA Review Council will reveal its reform recommendations on December 11th, potentially shifting disaster response costs. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth will travel to the Dominican Republic to strengthen defense ties. The EPA is abandoning its defense of a rule setting tough standards for soot pollution. Top U.S. military adviser General Dan Caine met with the prime minister of Trinidad and Tobago to discuss regional challenges. Trump announced plans to send envoys to meet with Putin and Ukrainian officials to advance his Russia-Ukraine peace deal. A coalition of 20 states is suing the Trump administration over proposed changes to homeless funding. Trump falsely claimed no murders in DC for six months, despite official homicide statistics. Trump pardoned Thanksgiving turkeys Gobble and Waddle, using the ceremony to criticize Chicago Mayor JB Pritzker and Joe Biden. He also expressed optimism about a Russia-Ukraine peace deal. Homeland Security defended the review of refugees, stating a need for stricter vetting. Louisiana Surgeon General Ralph Abraham, who ended vaccination promotion, has been selected for a top CDC post. Marjorie Taylor Greene's resignation surprised some in Georgia who believed she could have won without Trump's support. The DCCC is launching outreach efforts for rural communities and voters of color. A new survey indicates rising pessimism among U.S. Hispanics regarding the economy and immigration.
