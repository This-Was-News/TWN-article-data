## Article summary
Bad Bunny achieved a significant milestone at the 2025 Latin Grammy Awards in Las Vegas, securing five awards, including the coveted album of the year for his album DeBÍ TiRAR MáS FOToS. This marked his first win in the show's three major categories. He also triumphed in four urban and reggaeton categories. The artist, born Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio, expressed surprise and gratitude, dedicating his album of the year award to the youth of Latin America and Puerto Rico. Karol G won song of the year for "Si Antes Te Hubiera Conocido," and Alejandro Sanz took home record of the year for "Palmeras En El Jardín." The Argentine duo CA7RIEL & Paco Amoroso were also notable winners, receiving five awards for their album *Papota*. Paloma Morphy was recognized as best new artist. Legacy artists like Gloria Estefan, Fito Páez, and Rubén Blades also received awards. Brazilian singer Liniker, a previous Latin Grammy winner, won three awards for her project *Caju* and delivered a memorable performance. While most attendees focused on celebrating music, Los Tigres del Norte used their performance to highlight immigrant rights. Gloria and Emilio Estefan concluded their acceptance speech with a dedication to the global success of Latinos.
