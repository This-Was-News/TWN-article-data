## Article summary
A 16-year-old male student opened fire at Evergreen High School on Wednesday, injuring two others before dying from a self-inflicted gunshot wound. One victim remains in critical condition, while another has non-life-threatening injuries. The shooter used a revolver at 12:24 p.m., prompting a swift response from law enforcement. Three students, including the shooter, were treated at a local hospital, with a fourth seeking medical attention after escaping to a nearby elementary school. The sheriff's office received calls about an active shooter around 12:30 p.m. and encountered the shooter within five minutes, without discharging their weapons. Federal officials assisted with investigations and student interviews. Parents reunited with their children at Bergen Meadow Elementary School, and all students were accounted for by late Wednesday. The sheriff's office is seeking search warrants for the shooter's home and locker. Evergreen High School, about 28 miles southwest of Denver, enrolls around 900 students. All school activities were canceled for the day. Students and parents expressed shock and disbelief at the incident. The Jefferson County Education Association called for meaningful action to end gun violence in schools. Governor Jared Polis offered condolences and state assistance in the aftermath.
