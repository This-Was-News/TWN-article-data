## Article summary
Charlie Kirk, a prominent conservative figure and founder of Turning Point USA, died at age 31 after being shot during an event at Utah Valley University. Kirk, a staunch ally of President Trump, played a pivotal role in mobilizing young voters for Trump's 2024 election victory. He launched Turning Point USA at 18, aiming to counter progressive influences on college campuses and build a strong conservative youth movement. The organization now has over 800 college chapters and a significant online following. Kirk was known for his advocacy of free speech, free markets, and limited government, often criticizing liberal policies. He was also controversial, hosting figures and spreading conspiracy theories. At the time of his death, Kirk was on a college tour, engaging with students on various issues. Turning Point USA's advocacy wing, Turning Point Action, supported Trump's campaign, focusing on mobilizing voters of all ages. Kirk's impact on young voters was notable, contributing to Trump's gains among this demographic. His work was praised by Trump and other conservative leaders, who highlighted his influence on the 2024 election. Kirk is survived by his wife and two children.
