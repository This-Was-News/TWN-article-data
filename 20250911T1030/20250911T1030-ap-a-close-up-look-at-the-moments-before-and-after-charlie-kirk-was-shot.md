## Article summary
A large crowd gathered at Utah Valley University to hear conservative speaker Charlie Kirk, known for his provocative views and association with the Make America Great Again movement. Kirk was addressing the audience when a single gunshot interrupted his speech, striking him in the neck. Chaos ensued as the crowd fled in panic, with some slipping and falling. Witnesses described the terror and confusion that followed the shooting. Kirk, the founder of Turning Point USA, was known for his controversial statements on gender, race, religion, and politics. His campus appearances often drew protests, and this event was no different, with online petitions calling for its cancellation. Despite the controversy, the university affirmed its commitment to free speech. The shooter, clad in dark clothing, remained at large as authorities searched the area. The incident drew condemnation from across the political spectrum, highlighting the escalating threat of political violence in the United States. Kirk's death left a community in shock and mourning, with vigils and tributes held in his honor.
